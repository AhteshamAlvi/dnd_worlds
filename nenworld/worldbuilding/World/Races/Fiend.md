# Fiend

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]]

A Fiend begins as a death. Somewhere, a person dies afraid — of dogs, of guns, of the dark, of anything — and if a Devil takes enough interest in that fear, it can reshape the corpse into a new being built from the concept the person feared, wearing enough of that person's memories and personality to still recognizably be them. The result is a Fiend: not quite the person who died, not quite the Devil whose fear built them, something new stitched from both.

Fiends retain more of their humanity than the Devils they're built from — most can pass as ordinary people, hold jobs, have relationships, forget for long stretches what they actually are. But the fear at their core doesn't go away, and it tends to surface exactly when they're threatened, cornered, or pushed past their limit, at which point the Fiend's more monstrous, fear-shaped nature takes over.

Because Fiends are made rather than born, there's no Fiend homeland or population center — they turn up wherever a Devil happened to take an interest in a corpse, and most spend their existence hiding what they are from the humans around them.
