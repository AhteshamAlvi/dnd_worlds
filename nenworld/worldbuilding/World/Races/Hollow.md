# Hollow

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]]

Hollows are what's left of a soul that isn't properly laid to rest — a person who died with regret, attachment, or trauma so strong it corroded the soul into something hungry. In its rawest form a Hollow is a monster: a white mask over a ruined, animalistic body, driven by an emptiness it tries to fill by devouring other souls. Left alone, that's all a Hollow ever is.

But Hollows can evolve. Given enough time, power, and devoured souls, a Hollow sheds its monstrous shape and returns to something human-passing — a person again, mask reduced to a fragment worn somewhere on the body, mind intact, personality restored. This evolved state is called an **Arrancar**: someone who died once, came back wrong, and clawed their way back to being a person, more or less, with an unshakeable hunger and a piece of monster still riding along under the skin. Arrancar keep the raw power of what they were while regaining the intelligence to use it deliberately, which makes them by far the most dangerous of their kind.

Hollow society, such as it is, tends to be built on strength and hierarchy — the more souls a Hollow has consumed, the more power and standing it holds among its own kind. Among humans, Hollows and Arrancar are regarded with the wariness reserved for anyone who technically died once and is still hungry.

## Integrating Hollows without souls

If you don't want an afterlife, soul cosmology, or the shinigami/soul-reaper apparatus that usually comes attached to Hollows, they still work with one small reframe: treat a Hollow not as a departed soul but as a **living person consumed by a want they couldn't let go of** — grief, obsession, a hunger for revenge — that curdles into a physical hollowing-out while they're still, technically, alive. Under this version:

- A Hollow forms from a person overtaken by an all-consuming need, the same way an [[Infernal]] ignites from grief or a [[Spirits|Cursed Spirit]] condenses from fear — no death required, just a mind eaten out from the inside.
- What they "devour" isn't souls but the same raw emotional/[[Nen]] energy that everything else in this world runs on, taken from people or other Hollows.
- **Arrancar** remain the endpoint: a Hollow that has consumed enough to claw its personhood back, now permanently changed.

This keeps the Hollow → Arrancar arc, the mask, the hunger, and the hierarchy, while dropping the entire soul-and-afterlife layer and the shinigami who would otherwise police it.
