# Hollow

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]]

Hollows are what's left of a soul that isn't properly laid to rest — a person who died with regret, attachment, or trauma so strong it corroded the soul into something hungry. In its rawest form a Hollow is a monster: a white mask over a ruined, animalistic body, driven by an emptiness it tries to fill by devouring other souls. Left alone, that's all a Hollow ever is.

But Hollows can evolve. Given enough time, power, and devoured souls, a Hollow sheds its monstrous shape and returns to something human-passing — a person again, mask reduced to a fragment worn somewhere on the body, mind intact, personality restored. This evolved state is what most people mean when they picture a Hollow living among the population: someone who died once, came back wrong, and clawed their way back to being a person, more or less, with an unshakeable hunger and a piece of monster still riding along under the skin.

Hollow society, such as it is, tends to be built on strength and hierarchy — the more souls a Hollow has consumed, the more power and standing it holds among its own kind. Among humans, Hollows are regarded with the wariness reserved for anyone who technically died once and is still hungry.
