# Fullbringer

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]] › [[Humans]]

A Fullbringer is made before they're born. When a pregnant woman is attacked by a [[Hollow]] and survives, a trace of what attacked her can pass into the child — not enough to make the child anything other than human, but enough to leave a permanent mark on how their aura behaves. The result is someone who can reach into objects and pull out the latent aura sitting inside them.

Everything holds a little residual aura: a blade that's been carried for thirty years, a coat someone died in, the ground itself, water, a coin handled by a thousand people. A Fullbringer can draw that out and put it to work — sharpening a weapon far past what its metal should allow, hardening cloth into armour, walking on water by pulling at what's under their feet. The strength of the effect depends entirely on how much history the object carries, so a Fullbringer's most treasured possession is usually something worn, old, and personally significant rather than anything expensive.

Crucially, **Fullbringers are not related to each other in any way.** They share an origin story, not a bloodline — scattered individuals across the world whose mothers happened to survive the same kind of encounter, most of whom never meet another one and assume for years that whatever they can do is a private oddity. Those who do find each other tend to form small, tight, defensive groups, since almost no one else can explain what they are.
