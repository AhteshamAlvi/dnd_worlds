# Iburi

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]] › [[Humans]] › [[Clans]]

The Iburi turn to smoke. A member of the clan can convert all or part of their body into a dense grey vapour and back again — passing through gaps, taking blows that simply drift apart on impact, spreading out to fill a room and reforming somewhere else in it. Structurally it's the same kind of trait the [[Hozuki]] carry, one clan's body becoming water where the other's becomes smoke.

Where the Iburi differ from the Hozuki is control, and it is not a small difference. The transformation is only partly voluntary: Iburi bodies slip into smoke on their own, especially in childhood, during injury, illness, or strong emotion. Reassembling from that state takes deliberate concentration, and a member who disperses while unconscious, panicking, or too weak to focus may not come back together at all. The clan loses children this way. Every Iburi grows up knowing exactly how they are most likely to die.

That danger kept the clan small, insular, and largely hidden underground for generations — both figuratively and literally, since still air and enclosed spaces make it far easier to hold a dispersed body together than open wind does. Iburi who leave tend to be the ones who mastered control early, and they make superb infiltrators for obvious reasons.
