# Ubuyashiki

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]] › [[Humans]] › [[Clans]]

What the Ubuyashiki inherit is a death sentence. Every member of the clan is born with the same hereditary affliction: they sicken young, deteriorate steadily, and almost never live past thirty. It cannot be treated, cannot be trained around, and has never skipped a generation. Ubuyashiki children are raised knowing roughly how long they have, and the clan marries and bears children early out of pure necessity.

Alongside the illness comes the other half of the inheritance — an uncanny intuition that shades into genuine foresight. Ubuyashiki read people and situations with an accuracy that unsettles everyone around them, sense trouble coming well before there's anything to point at, and possess a calm, magnetic presence that makes even hardened, dangerous people inclined to listen. It is the rare inherited trait that is genuinely useless in a fight and enormously powerful everywhere else.

The combination made the clan into leaders rather than fighters. Ubuyashiki have historically sat at the centre of alliances, organisations, and long campaigns — coordinating people far stronger than themselves, choosing the right person for the right task with unnerving precision, and handing the whole apparatus to an heir before the illness finishes them. The clan's authority is built on the open fact that its head will be dead soon and is spending what's left deliberately.
