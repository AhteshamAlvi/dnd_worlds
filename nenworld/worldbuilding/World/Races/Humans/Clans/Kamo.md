# Kamo

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]] › [[Humans]] › [[Clans]]

Kamo blood answers to the will of the person it's inside. A member of the clan can move, shape, harden, and propel their own blood as easily as flexing a limb — drawing it out as a blade, flattening it into a shield, firing it as a spray of needles, or thickening and thinning it inside their own body to change how fast they clot, heal, or bleed out. It isn't a technique layered on top of the body; the blood itself is simply built differently.

The obvious limit is supply. Everything a Kamo does costs them blood they actually have, so the clan fights in careful, economical bursts and treats a long engagement as something close to a death sentence. Skilled members spend as much effort recovering and reabsorbing spent blood as they do attacking with it, and the truly dangerous ones are those who've learned to do more with less rather than those willing to bleed the most.

The clan's origin is usually given as [[Bloodkin]] work — an ancestor remade with the essence of something whose blood moved on its own — and their similarity to the [[Chinoike]], who manipulate the iron in blood through the eyes rather than the body, has fed centuries of argument over whether the two clans share a source. Neither family is fond of the comparison, which has done nothing to stop anyone making it.
