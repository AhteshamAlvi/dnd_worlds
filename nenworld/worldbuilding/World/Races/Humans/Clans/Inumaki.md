# Inumaki

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]] › [[Humans]] › [[Clans]]

Every Inumaki is born marked — dark sigils across the tongue and the inside of the mouth, present from birth and impossible to remove. The marks aren't decorative. An Inumaki's words carry compulsion: speak a command plainly and directly at someone, and their body obeys it before their mind gets a say. *Stop.* *Sleep.* *Get away from me.* The stronger the command relative to what the target's body can survive, the more it costs to give.

The cost is the clan's defining problem. Compulsion tears at the throat that produces it — a heavy command can leave an Inumaki bleeding from the mouth, voiceless for days, and a lifetime of overuse ruins the voice permanently. The result is a family that has built its entire culture around *not talking.* Inumaki speak in deliberately harmless substitute vocabulary among outsiders, communicate in gesture and writing where they can, and save actual plain speech for moments that are worth the damage.

That silence tends to be misread as coldness, and the clan has never been especially interested in correcting the impression — a reputation for being unapproachable is useful when any careless sentence in your ordinary speaking voice could seize hold of the person listening.
