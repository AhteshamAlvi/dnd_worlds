# Clans

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]] › [[Humans]]

A Clan is a named family line that matters — one small, traceable family tree whose members can all point back to the same ancestors, and whose name carries weight because of what that family holds: a trait in the blood, a technique nobody outside the family is taught, a title, a fortune, or a grudge.

Only some clans are [[Humans#Human Subtypes|Human Subtypes]]. The ones below marked as **bloodline clans** carry a genuine physical inheritance — something in the body itself that only the family has — and most of those are [[Bloodkin]] of some kind, the [[Fanalis]] being the clearest case. The rest are clans in the ordinary sense: powerful families, noble houses, and dynasties whose members are physically just human, and whose influence rests on secret techniques, wealth, rank, or history instead. Both kinds matter for worldbuilding; only the first kind changes what a person's body can do.

What separates any Clan from scattered [[Bloodkin]] like the [[Quincy]] or [[Pyrokinetic]] is containment — a Clan is one family, not a trait spread across many unrelated ones.

## Bloodline clans

Clans carrying a real, inherited bodily trait.

- [[Uchiha]] — Sharingan (the eyes physically change)
- [[Hyuga]] — Byakugan (eyes)
- [[Chinoike]] — Ketsuryūgan (eyes)
- [[Kurta]] — Scarlet Eyes; hunted to near-extinction for them
- [[Gojo]] — the Six Eyes; sees the structure of aura itself
- [[Kaguya]] — manipulate their own skeleton, bone as weapon
- [[Hozuki]] — bodies can turn to water
- [[Iburi]] — bodies turn to smoke, only partly under their control
- [[Kamo]] — their own blood moves at will
- [[Inumaki]] — born mouth-marked; their speech compels obedience
- [[Yuki]] — produce ice from the body; run cold, suffer in heat
- [[Jugo Clan|Jūgo Clan]] — absorb natural energy involuntarily; transform and rage
- [[Aburame]] — bodies host insect colonies from birth
- [[Inuzuka]] — feral physiology, animal-sharp senses
- [[Uzumaki]] — extreme vitality and longevity
- [[Ubuyashiki]] — hereditary illness and early death, paired with foresight
- [[Kurama Clan|Kurama Clan]] — illusions vivid enough to take physical effect
- [[Senju]]
- [[Fanalis]] — superhuman strength and speed, no Nen required (Bloodkin descended from the Red Lion)
- [[Vinsmoke]] — engineered lineage; Construct work that bred true

## Other clans and houses

Families whose power is in technique, rank, wealth, or history rather than the body.

### Naruto
- [[Nara]] — shadow-manipulation techniques, taught only within the family
- [[Yamanaka]] — mind-transfer and mental intrusion techniques
- [[Akimichi]] — body-expansion techniques fuelled by stored calories
- [[Sarutobi]] — an old, distinguished line of leaders and teachers
- [[Hatake]] — a small, respected family of exceptional individual talent
- [[Shimura]] — political operators; the hand behind a lot of quiet decisions
- [[Kamizuru]] — insect-handling rivals to the [[Aburame]], long in decline
- [[Fuma|Fūma]] — once an enormous shinobi clan, since splintered and scattered

### Jujutsu Kaisen
- [[Zenin]] — the third of the great sorcerer houses; brutally traditional, technique-obsessed

### Bleach
- [[Kuchiki]] — the most rigidly proper of the great noble houses
- [[Shihoin|Shihōin]] — a noble house tied to covert operations and stealth work
- [[Shiba]] — a once-great house fallen out of standing
- [[Ishida]] — the last family holding to the old [[Quincy]] traditions

### HunterXHunter
- [[Zoldyck]] — a dynasty of assassins who raise children into the trade from birth
- [[Nostrade]] — a criminal family trading in Nen users and rare goods

### One Piece
- [[Charlotte]] — an enormous dynasty built on marriage alliances and dozens of children
- [[Kozuki]] — the rightful ruling line of an isolated country
- [[Kurozumi]] — the usurping family that displaced them; a name treated as a curse
- [[Donquixote]] — descended from world nobility; some kept the title, some fled it
- [[Nefertari]] — a royal line that famously refused a seat among the world's rulers

### Black Clover
- [[Silva]] — a proud noble house of mercury magic
- [[Vermillion]] — a noble house of fire magic and ferocious tempers
- [[Kira]] — a noble house holding significant political rank
- [[Agrippa]] — a reclusive noble house with a grim reputation

### Demon Slayer
- [[Kamado]] — an ordinary mountain family carrying an extraordinary inherited technique
- [[Tsugikuni]] — the origin point of the oldest and strongest breathing style
- [[Rengoku]] — a hereditary line of flame-style masters

### Fairy Tail
- [[Heartfilia]] — a merchant dynasty of immense wealth
- [[Dreyar]] — three generations of guild leadership and its fallout

### My Hero Academia
- [[Todoroki]] — a family assembled deliberately to produce a stronger heir

### Magi: Kingdom of Magic
- [[Ren Family|Ren]] — the imperial family of an expansionist empire
- [[Saluja]] — a royal line of a trade kingdom, complicated by an unacknowledged heir
