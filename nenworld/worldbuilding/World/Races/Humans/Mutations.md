A Mutation is a one-off deviation from the human baseline — not a bloodline, not something passed down, not something anyone designed. It happens to one person, once, as the product of pure chance, which is exactly what separates it from everything else on this page. A [[Humans#Human Subtypes|Human Subtype]] is an inherited trait shared by a whole lineage; a [[Constructs|Construct]] is deliberate and repeatable, built the same way on purpose. A Mutation is neither — it's a roll of the dice that landed on exactly one person, and would almost certainly never land the same way twice.

## Causes

The causes are as varied as the chance behind them, and plenty have no discoverable cause at all:

- **Anomalous birth** — born different to two entirely ordinary parents, no explanation required. A person born with four arms, two mouths, and four eyes is a Mutation in exactly this sense: nothing in the bloodline predicted it, nothing repeated it.
- **Exposure** — eating a rare mineral, drinking contaminated water, handling a strange relic, being caught in a burst of raw aura or emotion far stronger than a body was built to survive.
- **Place** — falling into a strange lake, wandering into land warped by centuries of aura, curse, or grief soaked into the ground.
- **No cause at all** — some people are just born different, and no one, including them, ever finds out why.

## Not always an addition

A Mutation doesn't have to grant anything. Sometimes it's an absence instead — and the starkest example in this world is being born **completely unable to use Nen.** No aura nodes will ever open; no teacher, no technique, no amount of training will ever change it. Because virtually every human here is born with the innate capacity to become a Nen user, being entirely closed off from it is just as rare, and just as much a Mutation, as growing a second pair of arms. People born this way are often pushed to compensate — driving physical conditioning, weapon skill, or raw non-Nen craft far past anything a Nen user would ever need to bother with, because it's the only path left open to them, and some become monstrously good at exactly that.

## Range and inheritance

Mutations can be as visible as an extra limb or as invisible as a missing sense — physical, internal, cosmetic, functional, anywhere in between. Most stop with the person they happened to; a Mutation usually isn't heritable, because it was never a stable trait to begin with. In vanishingly rare cases one proves stable enough to breed true in a person's children — at which point it stops being called a Mutation at all, and becomes the seed of a brand new [[Humans#Human Subtypes|Human Subtype]] a few generations down the line.
