# Vampires

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]]

Vampires need to eat people — not metaphorically, but as a constant, gnawing hunger that only human flesh and blood can answer. In exchange they get physical strength far past anything a normal person could reach and regeneration fast enough to close most wounds almost as soon as they're taken. Every Vampire also manifests its own **Blood Demon Art**, a signature power tied to its own nature — separate and distinct from Nen. A Vampire can still train and develop a Nen ability on top of that, but the Blood Demon Art isn't one; it's part of what they are.

Killing one is not easy. Sunlight, beheading, and outright cellular destruction — intense flame, or anything else that reduces the body to nothing rather than just wounding it — are the only reliable ends. Silver burns and weakens most Vampires badly, though it's a minor irritant at best to an Original.

## Original Vampires

Originals are a true species, born rather than made. They're mostly without emotion — not incapable of feeling anything, since fear and rage still register — but they lack the capacity for real conscience or meaningful connection to anyone. Whatever warmth, grief, or affection an Original appears to show is mimicry, a hunting tool honed over centuries of living among humans, performed exactly as easily in the same breath as killing whoever fell for it.

An Original is immune to sunlight and mostly resistant to silver, though beheading and cellular destruction still kill it like any Vampire. An Original can create more Vampires by infecting a human with its blood, and every Vampire it creates is bound to it: if the Original dies, all of its Vampires die with it — with one exception (below).

## Vampires

A Vampire is a human transformed — infected with an Original's blood, after which the body stops aging, wounds close at unnatural speed, and the hunger for human flesh takes hold as a permanent, defining need. Most keep a real sliver of who they were: memories, mannerisms, personality, even the capacity for love or loyalty. A newly turned Vampire can still recognizably be someone's parent or friend, though that sliver erodes the longer they live this way and the more they give in to the hunger.

A Vampire is bound to the Original whose blood turned it and dies if that Original does. Sunlight is lethal, and silver is a real threat rather than a nuisance. But given enough time and enough humans eaten, a Vampire can grow past all of that — evolving into a **Made Original**: immune to sunlight, freed from the tether to whoever turned it, and resistant to silver like any Original. Unlike a born Original, a Made Original keeps everything it had as a Vampire — real emotion, real conscience, the capacity for connection — it simply becomes powerful enough to stop needing anyone else to answer to. Beheading still kills it all the same.
