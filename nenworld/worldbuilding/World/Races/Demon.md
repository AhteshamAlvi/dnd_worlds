# Demon

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]]

This Demon is a true species, not a transformed human — born of it, raised among it, indistinguishable from a human at a glance, and built entirely without emotion. What looks like a demon smiling, grieving, or begging for its life is mimicry: demons study human speech and behavior purely as a hunting tool, the way an anglerfish grows a lure, and they will use a perfectly convincing plea for mercy in the exact same breath as killing the person who fell for it.

This makes demons uniquely dangerous in a way that has nothing to do with raw power. A demon that has spent decades among humans can sound, by every surface measure, like a friend, a mentor, even a victim — and mean none of it, because there is no capacity underneath the performance for it to mean anything. Most humans who have spent real time around demons come away with a specific, hard-won instinct: fluent human warmth from something that has no reason to feel any is not proof of good faith, it's proof of a very old predator.

Demons live long — centuries, sometimes far longer — and mostly keep to themselves or to isolated territories, since a demon's presence among a human settlement rarely ends well for the settlement.
