# Elementals

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]]

An Elemental is natural energy alone, given shape and a will, with nothing human anywhere in it. That single fact places them precisely in the world's energy taxonomy: a [[Spirits|Spirit]] is [[Nen]] fused with natural energy and shaped by human emotion; a [[Hollow]] is a human spirit that natural energy gathered around; an Elemental is what natural energy does **by itself**, with no seed of a person involved at all.

They form where natural energy pools thick enough and long enough to hold a pattern — the heart of a volcano, a river that has run the same course for ten thousand years, a storm front that keeps returning to the same valley, a mountain's deep stone. What emerges takes on the character of the place that made it: fire, water, stone, wind, ice, and stranger things besides. An Elemental's body *is* the energy, so wounding one is closer to disrupting a current than injuring a creature, and a badly scattered Elemental will often simply reform given time and a source to draw on.

Because no human feeling went into making them, Elementals are the least humanlike thinking beings a person is likely to meet. They aren't cruel or kind; they want what the phenomenon they came from "wants" — to burn, to flow, to remain, to move — and they can be genuinely bewildered by requests that don't map onto that. Bargaining with one usually means finding the version of your goal that happens to run in the same direction as its nature. Older Elementals grow far more articulate and far more capable of abstraction, and the truly ancient ones are closer to local gods than creatures, treated by nearby settlements with offerings and careful respect.

Since Elementals possess real intelligence, they can develop [[Nen]] like any thinking being, and it comes to them unusually easily — they are already made of the raw material. What they almost never do is develop it in a *human* direction: an Elemental's abilities tend to be enormous, simple, and inseparable from what it already is, rather than the clever conditional constructions a human practitioner builds.
