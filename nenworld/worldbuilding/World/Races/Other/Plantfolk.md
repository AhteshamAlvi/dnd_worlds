# Plantfolk

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]]

Plantfolk are people grown rather than born. Their bodies are built from bark, heartwood, vine, fibre, and leaf instead of flesh, and they reproduce by seed — a Plantfolk child begins as something planted, tended, and waited on rather than carried. They are not animated plants and not [[Constructs]]; nobody made them, they simply grow the way any other people is simply born.

They run on a different clock than everyone around them, and that shapes nearly everything about them. Plantfolk take years to reach maturity, live for centuries, heal slowly but almost completely, and are notoriously unhurried in conversation and decision alike — a Plantfolk asked an urgent question may well answer it properly next season. Their bodies want sunlight, water, and good soil, so a Plantfolk cut off from all three weakens the way anyone else starves. Fire is the thing they genuinely fear, and the only injury most of them cannot simply outlast.

The largest of them are the **Treants**: enormous, tree-bodied elders who have generally stopped moving much at all, taking root somewhere and remaining for so long that whole forests, roads, and villages grow up around them without ever realising the landmark is a person. A Treant is usually the oldest thinking thing in its region, holds a memory of the area stretching back further than any written record, and can be an extraordinary source of information for anyone patient enough to wait out the pauses.

Plantfolk settlements are groves rather than towns, tended cooperatively and defended fiercely, and their politics tend to revolve around land, water, and grievances that other peoples forgot generations ago and they did not. They learn [[Nen]] readily — their patience makes them formidable at the long, disciplined work of it — and Plantfolk practitioners are disproportionately drawn to abilities built on [[Conditions]] and [[Vows and Limitations]], since a vow measured in decades costs them far less than it would cost anyone else.
