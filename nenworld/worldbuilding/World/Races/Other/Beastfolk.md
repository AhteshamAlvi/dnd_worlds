# Beastfolk

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]]

Beastfolk are humanoid peoples who carry the traits of an animal — anywhere from a faint set of features to a fully animal body. "Beastfolk" isn't one race so much as an enormous spectrum, spanning how much animal a given lineage actually shows, and where a people falls on that spectrum shapes how they live: the subtler kinds pass easily among humans, the fuller kinds don't, and the rarest kinds don't hold a single shape at all.

Roughly from least animal to most:

- **Marked** — very nearly human, distinguished only by a set of animal ears, a tail, sharper canines, and heightened senses. The standard fantasy "beast-eared" type; they live among ordinary humans with little trouble, sometimes without anyone noticing.
- **Half-beasts** — visibly part-animal: fur or scales over part of the body, clawed hands, a muzzle or snout, digitigrade legs, horns or feathers. Clearly not human, but still built on a human frame.
- **Full Beastfolk** — a complete animal's body walking upright, fully furred or scaled or feathered, animal head and all. The **Mink** are the archetype here: proud, martial tribes that come in as many varieties as there are animals. A Mink is a natural warrior before it ever trains.
- **Winged Beastfolk** — small, agile fliers, the best known being cat-like companions roughly the size of a child, capable of flight and often bonded to a partner for life. *(Exceed)*
- **Shifters** — lineages that don't hold one form. Some shift between a human shape and a beast one, whether at will or triggered by injury, rage, or the moon, in the werewolf mold. Others, like the **Kiriko**, can take a flawless human form to move unseen and drop it when it's time to fight.

Most Beastfolk are born fixed in whatever they are, their animal nature set from birth; only the shifter lineages actually change shape. Whatever their type, a Beastfolk can go on to train [[Nen]] on top of the gifts its body already gives it.
