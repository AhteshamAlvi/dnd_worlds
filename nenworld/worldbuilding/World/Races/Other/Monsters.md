# Monsters

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]]

Monsters are the natural predators of the world that were never human and never civilized. A vast, varied category of inhuman creatures that live to hunt, spread, and grow. Some are little more than dangerous animals; others are cunning enough to mimic human speech and behavior as a lure, feeling nothing underneath it, using a convincing cry for help in the same breath as the kill. What they share is that there is no common ground to be found with them.

Monsters range from vermin a village can handle to individuals powerful enough to level regions and outlive empires. The strongest are less "creatures" than forces of nature, and a single ancient Monster can define the history of everything living near it.

The **Tailed Beasts** are the definitive example of that upper extreme. They are the collective name for nine titanic Monsters, each an ocean of power in a single body that were, according to the oldest accounts, once a single primordial Monster that split apart into nine. Each of the nine is a country level threat in its own right, and their scattering across the world is treated less as a fact of natural history than as a recurring catastrophe.

Like animals, Monsters run overwhelmingly on natural energy rather than [[Nen]] — natural energy is what actually shapes most of them and fuels most of their power, and it's rare for a Monster to develop real Nen at all. When it happens, it happens the same way it does for any other creature: only once a Monster claws its way to genuine intelligence does it begin developing Nen of its own, sometimes alongside enough humanlike intelligence and speech to go with it (see [[Nen and Nature]]).

## Awakened Beasts

A rare few make it all the way across. An **Awakened Beast** is an animal or Monster that gained real intelligence — and, having gained it, stopped being something you can only fight. They speak, they reason, they hold grudges and keep bargains, and the defining line between them and everything else on this page is that **an Awakened Beast can be negotiated with.** Where an ordinary Monster offers no common ground at all, an Awakened one has interests, and interests can be traded against.

They arrive there by different roads. Some are ordinary animals that developed [[Nen]] and grew into intelligence alongside it, as [[Nen and Nature]] describes — animals born with strange mutations are especially prone to it. Others are Monsters that survived long enough and grew powerful enough that intellect came as a side effect of sheer accumulated strength. Either way, once the intellect is there, so is the capacity for genuine Nen, including [[Hatsu]] and abilities of their own design — the thing no unintelligent creature can ever manage.

Awakened Beasts are not a single people and share nothing but the fact of having woken up. Some live entirely alone, indistinguishable from an ordinary predator until they open their mouths and answer you. Others have built whole societies of their own kind — the great clans of enormous toads, slugs, snakes, and similar creatures that keep territories, hierarchies, elders, and long institutional memories, and that will occasionally enter into standing pacts with human families or settlements, lending their strength across generations in exchange for something they want. Many raise their young into Nen deliberately, the same way any people teaches its children, which is how an awakened line stays awake instead of sliding back into instinct.

Their relationship with humanity is entirely case-by-case. An Awakened Beast may be an ally, a landlord demanding tribute for crossing its territory, a scholar, a rival, or a monster that simply happens to be able to explain exactly why it's going to eat you.
