Merfolk are the peoples of the sea — humanoid above, adapted for the water below, able to breathe and move through the deep as easily as a human walks on land. The classic Merfolk are fish-tailed, but the category is broad and interbreeding is common, so the line between subtypes is blurry and a single family can produce children of more than one kind.

The most notable subtype are the **Fishman**: fully bipedal, humanlike in shape but built from a fish or shark or ray or eel, keeping gills, fins, and scales. Fishmen walk on land without trouble and are staggeringly strong — many times a human's power on dry ground, and more still underwater, where they can turn the sea itself into a weapon. A Fishman and a fish-tailed Merfolk can have children together freely; which traits a child inherits is largely chance.

Not every sea-people lives beneath the waves. The **Imuchakk** are a towering, cold-water branch of Merfolk — in fact a hybrid people, the descendants of [[Giant]] and Merfolk unions, standing half again to twice a human's height, blue-haired and powerfully built. They make their home on the frozen northern seas and hunt the monsters of the deep from the surface rather than swimming among them, carrying the Giant's raw size and the Merfolk's affinity for water in one frame. Their culture is built around the hunt as a rite of passage.

Merfolk range across every sea creature imaginable, which means their people range from gentle and reclusive to as dangerous as the predators they resemble. Like any race here, a Merfolk can also learn [[Nen]].
