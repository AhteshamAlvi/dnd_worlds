## What a Hollow is

Not every death leaves a Hollow behind. When someone dies, their [[Nen]] ordinarily dissolves and fades away, the same way any unattended aura eventually does. But if the death itself wasn't caused by a [[Nen Ability]] — nothing that unmakes aura directly, just an ordinary end: a blade, an illness, a fall, old age — and the person died consumed by a desire or regret so total it wouldn't let go, that lingering spirit becomes a seed instead of fading. Natural energy gathers around it, the way it gathers around anything charged with feeling strong enough to hold shape, and the two combine — [[Nen]] and natural energy fused together, the same basic recipe behind a [[Spirits|Spirit]] — falling deep underground into the caverns where Hollows form, and taking shape there as a Hollow: hunger and a single, specific desire given a body, the exact desire that consumed the person at the moment they died.

Hollows are born in those deep caverns and mostly stay there. Reaching the surface takes real effort and doesn't happen often — most people live their whole lives without a Hollow ever reaching them. When one does break through, it hunts humans directly, preying on the [[Nen]] of the living rather than the dead. Because a Hollow is made of aura rather than flesh, it's invisible to anyone who can't perceive it — which means every human capable of using [[Nen]] can see a Hollow on sight, while anyone else has no warning at all until it's too late.

## Nen and natural energy

Most Hollows, like most [[Monsters]], run almost entirely on natural energy rather than [[Nen]]. The human spirit at a Hollow's center supplies the seed — the grief, the desire, the shape — but the bulk of what a Hollow actually *is*, especially near the bottom of the ladder below, is natural energy given hunger and a mask. True Nen, the deliberate kind a person trains into, only comes from real intelligence (see [[Nen and Nature]]), and most Hollows simply don't have any yet. It's only once a Hollow claws its way to genuine intellect — Adjuchas and above — that it begins developing real Nen of its own. Even before that point, an unintelligent Hollow may pick up the [[Four Major Principles]] by pure instinct, the same way a wild animal does, and a rare one manages a basic intermediate technique the same instinctive way. What instinct alone can never produce is a [[Hatsu]] — that takes the kind of deliberate intelligence nothing below Adjuchas actually has.

## Hollow techniques

Because a Hollow's body is built from natural energy rather than trained [[Nen]], it comes with a handful of techniques already built in — instinctive expressions of what it's made from, available even to the least intelligent Hollow on the ladder below, no training or intellect required:

- **Cero** — a concentrated blast of natural energy, fired from wherever a Hollow chooses to focus it: the mouth, a palm, a horn. The signature Hollow attack, and its raw force scales with the size and strength of the Hollow firing it rather than with any skill behind it.
- **Sonído** — a burst of natural energy released through the legs that closes the distance between one point and another almost instantly. It isn't finesse so much as a controlled explosion in one direction, but at speed it's indistinguishable from teleportation to anyone who isn't watching closely.
- **Garganta** — natural energy torn open into a passage between two points, most commonly between the deep caverns where Hollows form and the surface above. Ripping one open takes real strength, which is exactly why surface breaches are rare: most Hollows simply aren't strong enough to tear the passage open in the first place.
- **Kūmon** — a cruder, larger-scale version of the same tear, used by Gillian-class Menos to move their whole mass at once rather than the tighter, more controlled Garganta an individual Hollow manages alone. A Kūmon opening is a rarer and far more dangerous event than an ordinary Garganta breach, since it means something Gillian-sized is coming through.

One phenomenon isn't a technique so much as a property of the caverns themselves: a Hollow gravely wounded but not finished off is occasionally reclaimed by the depths before it can be destroyed for good, dragged back down and left to recover in the same natural energy that made it. Nothing performs this on purpose and nothing can be relied on to trigger it — it simply happens, sometimes, to a Hollow that's earned enough strength for the depths to bother reclaiming it.

## Strength and evolution

How dangerous a newly formed Hollow is depends entirely on how strong the person was in life. Someone who was never much of a fighter comes out weak, at the very bottom of the ladder below. Someone who was formidable in life can emerge far up that same ladder immediately, sometimes skipping most of it outright. What follows is that ladder, bottom to top.

**Hollow.** The baseline. A newly formed Hollow driven by hunger and its one desire and little else — barely more than an animal, wearing a white mask over a body that's lost whatever shape it had in life, running on natural energy with no real Nen behind it.

**Huge Hollow.** Aside from scale, a Huge Hollow is no different from an ordinary one — same mind, same single-minded hunger, still running on natural energy, just bigger and more dangerous, the result of an ordinary Hollow eating enough wandering [[Nen]] and weaker prey to outgrow its own body.

**Menos Grande.** Eventually a Hollow's hunger outgrows what wandering remnants and easy prey can fill. When that happens, it turns on other Hollows instead — and cannibal Hollows are drawn to each other, ending in a mass merger of hundreds of them into a single towering entity far stronger than any of its parts. This fused existence comes in three grades:

- **Gillian** — the first and by far the most common result of a merger: an enormous, slow, mostly mindless mass, more crowd than individual, with beast-like intellect and immense raw power but no plan beyond hunger, and still nothing that amounts to real Nen. Most Gillians never become anything else. Occasionally, though, one of the many Hollows folded into the mass seizes control of the whole — and only that kind of Gillian can evolve further.
- **Adjuchas** — a Gillian that seized control and kept devouring, including its own fellow Hollows and Gillians, shrinking back down in size while growing sharply in intelligence and individual strength. This is the point on the ladder where real intellect finally arrives, and with it, real [[Nen]] — an Adjuchas is the weakest kind of Hollow that can actually be called a Nen user rather than a creature running on instinct and natural energy alone. Adjuchas are far rarer than Gillians, solitary rather than crowd-like, and often the ones directing Gillians in a fight rather than joining their ranks. The climb is precarious: an Adjuchas that stops consuming reverts back into a mindless Gillian, and one that's ever wounded badly enough to be partly devoured by another Hollow stops evolving permanently, frozen at whatever strength — and whatever Nen — it had reached.
- **Vasto Lorde** — the rarest and strongest of the three, and the peak of what a Hollow can become through the merger-and-consumption path: human-sized again, possessing a real self — identity, memory, deliberate will, a mind that plans instead of just hungers — and real command of [[Nen]] to go with it. A person powerful enough in life can also emerge here directly, without ever passing through a merger at all; strength earned in life and strength clawed back through consumption both end at the same place.

**Arrancar.** A vanishingly small number of Vasto Lorde go further still, breaking or removing their mask and shedding the Hollow shape entirely to return to something human-passing — a person again, in most ways, with the hunger and the desire riding along underneath. Arrancar keep the raw power of everything they consumed to get there, paired with the intelligence and full Nen mastery to use it deliberately, which makes them by far the most dangerous Hollows there are, and by far the rarest.

## Hollow society

Such society as Hollows have is built on strength and hierarchy, following the ladder above from ordinary Hollow up through Gillian, Adjuchas, and Vasto Lorde to the Arrancar at the very top. Among humans, the rare Hollow that reaches the surface — let alone a Menos or an Arrancar — is regarded with the wariness reserved for anything that died once, kept its hunger, and is still hunting.
