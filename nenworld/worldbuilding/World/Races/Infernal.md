# Infernal

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[World Building]] › [[Race]]

An Infernal isn't born, they're ignited — a person overwhelmed by grief, rage, or trauma can combust from the inside out and emerge as something built from fire instead of flesh. Most Infernals lose themselves completely in the process: personality gone, memories gone, nothing left but flame and instinct given exactly enough humanoid shape to be recognized as having once been a person. It's this state that most people mean when they say "Infernal," and it's treated as a tragedy rather than a monster attack, even when it has to be put down as one.

A rare few Infernals resist full dissolution and hold on to a fragment of who they were — enough to still recognize loved ones, still speak, still choose. These lucid Infernals are exceptionally uncommon, live with the fire as a permanent part of their body rather than something they can set aside, and exist in a strange social space: not fully accepted as people by those who only know the mindless kind, but not willing to give up what's left of themselves either.

Because becoming an Infernal is something that happens to a person rather than a lineage they're born into, there's no Infernal homeland or population — only individuals, scattered wherever grief burned hot enough.
