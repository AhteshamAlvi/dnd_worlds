# Ryū

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[Nen]] › [[Advanced Techniques]]

**Ryū** ("Flow") is a combination of *[[Gyō|Gyo]]*, *[[Ken]]*, *[[Chū]]*, and *[[Shū]]* (although *[[Shū]]* is optional). It is the term for use of *[[Gyō|Gyo]]* while in states of *[[Ken]]* and *[[Chū]]* to perform real-time offensive and defensive value adjustments. If *[[Kō]]* dedicates 100% of one's aura to offense or, much more rarely, to defense, *[[Ken]]* splits it evenly between the two, and *[[Chū]]* directs it inwards as well as outwards, *Ryū* is redistributing one's aura according to any other percentage (e.g., by focusing 70% of one's aura in one fist and 30% everywhere else as one is about to strike, or 80% in one's leg and 20% everywhere else to block an incoming kick) both internally and externally, either together or independent of each other (e.g. focusing 40% of aura outside one fist, 20% inside the fist and arm, and 40% to cover the rest of the body). It is utilized to damage a *[[Ken]]* user without leaving oneself as unprotected as during *[[Kō]]*, although the power of the technique is lower.

*Ryū* is difficult to master, as it requires the user to not only control one's aura flow with great precision, but also be able to estimate the amount of aura utilized by the opponent at a glance. Even if the technique is performed correctly, the movements of the aura flow risk giving away the user's next move if they are too slow, although with true mastery, complex movements across the body can be used to confound the enemy while maintaining delicate movements in battle.
