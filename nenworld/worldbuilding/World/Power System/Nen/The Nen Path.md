# The Nen Path

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[Nen]]

Everyone starts as an ordinary person with no command of their aura at all. Becoming a Nen user means first walking a shared **foundation** — a handful of steps almost everyone takes in the same rough order, because each one is built out of the last. After that foundation, the Path stops being a ladder and becomes a **branch**: which techniques a practitioner picks up, when they craft their signature ability, and how far they push in any direction is personal. Two skilled Nen users can have very different, incomplete sets of techniques and both be formidable.

The [[Nen Path|Nen Path canvas]] shows the techniques as a map; each stop links to its full write-up.

## The shared foundation

These come in roughly this order for nearly everyone, because each genuinely depends on the one before it.

**Tier 0 — Ordinary.** No control over aura. It leaks constantly (see [[Aura]]) and is invisible to them; sensitive people only feel it as a vague presence. Everyone begins here.

**Tier 1 — Awakening.** The aura nodes are forced or coaxed open — slowly through meditation, or fast and risky through [[Initiation]]. The person can now *sense* and *leak* aura in force, but can't yet control it, and is dangerously exhausted until they can. This is the doorway, not the destination.

**Tier 2 — The [[Four Major Principles]].** The floor of being a real Nen user, and the one part that really is learned in order:
- [[Ten]] — contain your aura, stop the leak, form a shroud.
- [[Zetsu]] — shut the flow off entirely; rest, hide, or sharpen sensitivity.
- [[Ren]] — pour out aura in volume; the engine behind every offensive use.
- [[Hatsu]] — your personal expression of aura, the seed everything individual grows from.

**Tier 3 — Discovery.** Through [[Water Divination]], the practitioner learns which of the six [[Types of Nen]] they were born to. This sets their affinities — what will come easily, what will fight them — and colours everything they build afterward. Most find this out early, but some fight for years before they ever test it.

## Beyond the foundation: your own branch

Once the Four Principles are in hand, there is **no fixed order left.** The [[Intermediate Techniques]], the [[Advanced Techniques]], and a personal [[Nen Ability]] are picked up in whatever sequence a practitioner's talent, teacher, temperament, and need dictate:

- **The signature ability comes when it comes.** Some practitioners craft their [[Nen Ability]] early, almost as soon as they know their type, and grow into it for the rest of their lives. Others drill techniques for years and only later distil an ability out of everything they've learned. Neither is wrong — see [[Developing a Nen Ability]].
- **The [[Intermediate Techniques]] are a toolkit, not a checklist.** [[Gyō]], [[In]], [[En]], [[Shū]], [[Ken]], [[Chū]] — most users learn several, few learn all of them, and which ones stick depends on what their fighting and their ability actually call for.
- **You can reach for an [[Advanced Techniques|advanced technique]] without collecting every intermediate first.** [[Kō]], [[Ryū]], [[Fū]], [[Jū]], [[Yū]] each build on the Four Principles and on *specific* skills — not on the whole intermediate roster. Someone can learn [[Yū]] without ever learning [[Ken]], if Yū is what their path demands and they have what Yū itself requires.

**Mastery**, then, isn't a final tier so much as a state: the principles run without thought (a master holds [[Ten]] even while asleep), the signature ability has grown extra forms and stages deepened by [[Vows and Limitations]] and [[Conditions]], and the practitioner's particular branch has grown deep. Growth never really stops; it just stops being about the basics.

## The real gate: dependency, not sequence

There is still a hard rule — it's just about *dependency*, not tier order: **a practitioner can only build on what they have actually learned.** A new technique, a new form of an ability, a new stage — each can only lean on the specific things the user already has. Someone who has never learned [[En]] cannot have an ability that quietly senses a room. An advanced technique that flows aura a certain way still needs the Four Principles solid underneath it. What you *don't* need is everything in between: skip what your path doesn't call for, so long as you actually hold whatever the next thing genuinely rests on.
