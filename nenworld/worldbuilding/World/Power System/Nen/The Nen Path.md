# The Nen Path

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[Nen]]

Everyone starts as an ordinary person with no command of their aura at all. Becoming a Nen user is a road walked in a fixed order — each step is *learned*, and no step can be skipped, because every later technique is built out of the earlier ones. This page lays that road out as a single journey; the [[Nen Path|Nen Path canvas]] shows the same thing as a map, and each stop links to its full write-up.

The order in which techniques are **first learned** is fixed. How much a practitioner keeps training each one afterward is not — a veteran drills [[Ten]] and [[Ren]] daily for life, long after moving on to advanced work.

## The tiers

**Tier 0 — Ordinary.** No control over aura. It leaks constantly (see [[Aura]]) and is invisible to them; sensitive people only feel it as a vague presence. Everyone begins here.

**Tier 1 — Awakening.** The aura nodes are forced or coaxed open — slowly through meditation, or fast and risky through [[Initiation]]. The person can now *sense* and *leak* aura in force, but can't yet control it, and is dangerously exhausted until they can. This is the doorway, not the destination.

**Tier 2 — Foundation (the [[Four Major Principles]]).** The floor of being a real Nen user. Learned in order:
- [[Ten]] — contain your aura, stop the leak, form a shroud.
- [[Zetsu]] — shut the flow off entirely; rest, hide, or sharpen sensitivity.
- [[Ren]] — pour out aura in volume; the engine behind every offensive use.
- [[Hatsu]] — your personal expression of aura, the seed everything individual grows from.

**Tier 3 — Discovery.** Through [[Water Divination]], the practitioner learns which of the six [[Types of Nen]] they were born to. This sets their affinities — what will come easily, what will fight them — and points their whole path.

**Tier 4 — Refinement (the [[Intermediate Techniques]]).** Finer control and utility built on the foundation: [[Gyō]], [[In]], [[En]], [[Shū]], [[Ken]], [[Chū]]. These widen what a user can *do* with aura before they ever commit to a signature ability.

**Tier 5 — Expression (the Nen Ability).** The practitioner crafts their own [[Nen Ability]] — the thing that makes them *them*. This is not handed out; it is grown. See [[Developing a Nen Ability]].

**Tier 6 — Advanced (the [[Advanced Techniques]]).** High-level combinations that demand the rest as a base: [[Kō]], [[Ryū]], [[Fū]], [[Jū]], [[Yū]]. Most are ways of concentrating, splitting, or flowing aura that only make sense once the foundation is automatic.

**Tier 7 — Mastery.** The principles run without thought — a master holds [[Ten]] even while asleep. The signature ability has grown extra forms and stages, deepened by [[Vows and Limitations]] and [[Conditions]]. Growth never really stops; it just stops being about the basics.

## Why the order is the gate

The single most important rule of the Path, and the one that keeps everything grounded: **a practitioner can only build on what they have actually learned.** A new form of an ability, a new trick, a new stage — none of it can lean on a technique the user hasn't reached yet. Someone who has never learned [[En]] cannot have an ability that quietly senses a room; someone still shaky on [[Ren]] has no business with [[Ryū]]. Power is earned upward through the tiers, never granted sideways.
