# Types of Nen

[[Ideal Power System For the Real World]] › [[Nen]]

There are six types of aura; [[Enhancement]], [[Transmutation]], [[Emission]], [[Conjuration]], [[Manipulation]], and [[Specialization]]. Every individual is born having one of these six different aura types. Upon learning one's own aura type, a student of Nen can set about learning to apply the technique in a unique way that suits them, which can develop into a unique skill.

![[nen-category-chart.png]]

There are numerical approximations to indicate exactly how efficient a Nen user would be at learning and utilizing categories into which they were not born. With the exception of Specialization, ease of learning (often called "affinity") and efficiency in output (also referred to as Force and Accuracy) coincide. Starting at one's own aura type, a Nen user other than a Specialist has the potential to master and is 100% efficient at using abilities based in that category alone. Then, looking at the category chart (see above), one is 80% efficient at using aura types adjacent to one's primary type, 40% efficient at the category opposite of their own, and 60% efficient in the two remaining categories. On top of expressing the efficiency of a Nen user's command over a certain given Nen type, these percentages also reflect how easy abilities from those Nen types are to develop for that nen user. However these are merely approximations on average. In reality there exist many combinations of affinities, and even people with multiple strange affinities to different Nen types. It is very varied. Leanings toward different affinities (including towards one’s own affinity) are not set in stone and can change based on a Nen user's training, environment, mindset, and other factors, although this is very rare and one rarely shifts their Nen type too far from their original, especially once it is significantly established.

Specialization is an exception to these rules. This Nen type is impossible to partially use: one either can or cannot use Specialist abilities, and only individuals who are Specialists can create Specialist abilities. However, turning into a Specialist later in life is possible, with Conjurers and Manipulators having the highest chance of succeeding. This facility is supposedly why Specialization is placed where it is in the Nen chart, although its placement there also affects affinity rates for other Nen types. Unlike other Nen users, Specialists also have 100% affinity for all Nen types, rather than only their own; however, it seems that their efficiency for other Nen types is not 100%. It is once again, varied depending on the Specialist.

It is best to base a Nen ability on one's individual aura type. Though it is possible for one to develop abilities outside of one's primary aura type, one is typically less proficient at using those abilities. By creating an ability that one isn't suited for, one risks stagnating their potential by overloading their capacity for using Nen. However, it is very possible, and even somewhat common, to reach a high level in multiple categories and utilize them consistently in primary Nen abilities. In fact, training in all the accessible categories is the fastest and most efficient way to improve one's skills in one's own Nen type, with the additional benefit of increasing one's flexibility and proficiency in Nen in general. The ideal training regimen is a bell curve, with the time spent on each category decreasing the farther away it is from the user's own type.
