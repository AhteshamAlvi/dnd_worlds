# Social, Exploration, and Investigation

[[00 Read Me First|Rulebook]] › GM Tools

The noncombat systems. One engine throughout ([[Core Resolution]]): intent → leverage → check → consequence. Nen bends every procedure here somewhere — and each section names where, and where it *can't*.

## Social play

**Structure per attempt:** the player states intent and approach; the GM names stakes and the NPC's **attitude** (Hostile / Wary / Neutral / Warm / Devoted); leverage adjusts position; then one check (Persuasion, Deception, Intimidation, Performance) vs opposed Insight or a DC by attitude (Hostile 20 · Wary 16 · Neutral 13 · Warm 10).

- **Leverage before dice:** a real favor owed, a secret held, a credible threat, a gift that costs — each shifts effective attitude one step (max two). Playing leverage *is* the game; the roll is the residue.
- **Results move NPCs one step** per success (two on a 10+ margin), and never past what the leverage supports: no roll talks a Registry auditor into treason on charm alone.
- **PCs are never dice-puppeted** ([[Injury Recovery and Conditions]]) — social results against PCs yield information, pressure, and offers. [[Types and Affinity (Rules)|Manipulation]]-type Nen is the deliberate exception, priced and criminalized ([[Society Law and Licenses]]).
- **Nen at the table's edge:** bloodlust-[[Ren]] is Intimidation's nuclear option (and an assault charge in front of witnesses); lie-detection exists **only as designed abilities with [[Conditions]]** (there is no generic truth-sense — the [[Hatsu Library|Patient Ledger]] shows the shape: bounded questions, exploitable terms); [[En]] reads a crowd's *texture* (agitation, fear, a Zetsu-quiet hole in the field) but never words or thoughts.

**Reputation (Renown 0–5, per region or faction):** earned by witnessed deeds and filed work; each rank opens doors (audiences, prices, benefit of the doubt) and closes others (anonymity, deniability, quiet exits). Track it like a stat; spend it like leverage.

**Faction favor clocks (0–6):** each named faction holds a clock per party; fill segments with services, drain with slights. At 4+, patronage; at 0, problems. This is the mechanical spine of [[Campaign Frameworks|intrigue arcs]].

## Investigation

**The three-clue spine:** every conclusion the plot needs has ≥3 discoverable routes to it. Checks never gate the *spine* — they gate speed, cost, and side rewards ([[Running the Game]]).

**Aura forensics** (the licensed profession — [[Society Law and Licenses]]):
| Task | Check & DC |
|---|---|
| Read residue, hours old | WIS Aura Sense / Gyō, DC 13 |
| Residue, days old | DC 16 (a week: DC 20) |
| Identify effect's category from traces | INT Nen Theory, DC 16 |
| Match a known user's signature | DC 20, requires prior read of that user's Ren |
| Deduce an ability's [[Conditions|Condition]] from repeated effects | Nen Theory DC 20, evidence from 2+ scenes — **the crown jewel; see hidden-info rules** |
| Notice a scene is Zetsu-*clean* (an absence where residue belongs) | Aura Sense DC 20 — and that absence is itself a signature |

**Interrogation:** contests (Insight vs Deception, Intimidation vs Composure), leverage rules apply; Nen shortcuts are the crime the interrogator's license dies by.
**Tailing:** opposed Stealth vs Perception in legs (crowd, transit, threshold); the aura layer runs separately (Zetsu vs Aura Sense) — a tail can pass one layer and fail the other, which is how professionals get made.
**Research:** a project (below) against archive quality: street rumor (1 pt/day, unreliable), guild records (2), Sella Polytechnic or Registry files (3, access required), the Deep Doors' texts (5, and the access *is* the arc).

## Travel and exploration

**Journey legs** (a day or a stage): navigator's WIS Survival vs terrain DC (road 5 · wild 13 · trackless 16 · Hollowlands 20); on a failure the leg costs double supply or lands in the region's complication table. Each leg: one supply tick, one encounter check (region-rated), one camp choice.

**Pace by SR** (overland km/day, sustainable): SR 1 · 30 km — SR 2 · 50 — SR 3 · 90 — SR 4+ · terrain and fuel decide (aura Drive across a day costs real pool; the courier trade prices routes in aura, not distance).

**Supply clock (d6 per party per leg):** tick down on bad legs, harsh weather, extra mouths; at 0, Endurance checks and foraging legs (Survival DC by biome). Boring on purpose to track, decisive on purpose to ignore.

**Camps — the Zetsu dilemma** (the setting's signature camping rule): sleeping in [[Zetsu]] recovers aura ×3 and hides you from everything aura-sensed — *and you are defenseless and blind to the aura layer.* Watch rotations, alarm perimeters (a tripwire [[Hatsu Library|Razor Psalm]], a 2 m [[En]] on the sentry) become real decisions. Aura-dense zones ([[Injury Recovery and Conditions]]) double recovery and tax the mind daily.

**Discovery pays:** first-contact finds (a region, a ruin, a people, a live Elemental) are TP events ([[Progression and Training]]) and Renown events. Exploration is a career, not a transition.

## Projects (research, crafting, building)

One frame for all long work: **Project = threshold points · a check per interval · quality of tools/site as multiplier** (mirrors [[Progression and Training]] training programs — one bookkeeping idiom everywhere).
Crafting: DEX or INT Craft; masterwork results grant +1 circumstance to their specific use (never stacking past +2). Nen-integrated gear: [[Nen Techniques (Rules)|Shū]]-tuned weapons (fitted to a user's flow: +1 accuracy for them alone), conjured-notarized documents, warded rooms (a hired Conjurer's rule-effect, priced as services — [[Society Law and Licenses]]).

## Criminal operations

Heists run in four phases — **casing** (investigation rules), **approach** (social/stealth legs), **execution** (the set-piece; In/Zetsu layers make Nen heists three-dimensional), **heat**. **Heat clock (0–6, per city):** fill with witnesses, residue, Renown backfiring; at 4+, response escalates a band; at 6, the [[The World of Sorane|city]] is done with you. Aura forensics cuts both ways — the crew's Zetsu discipline is its real skill floor.

## Contests and examinations

Structured rivalry (exams, tournaments' non-combat legs, license practica): best-of-N opposed legs across *different* skills, declared in advance, one leg per contestant chosen by their rival (the petty heart of every great exam). Full arc structure: [[Campaign Frameworks]] §1–2.

## What Nen must never shortcut

The standing GM line, one sentence per pillar: no ability reads minds without conditions an opponent could learn and beat; no ability wins a negotiation — it wins *position*; no ability replaces the three-clue spine — it accelerates one route. Nen abilities in noncombat play are *lenses and levers, never verdicts* — that's the design contract that keeps mysteries, markets, and courts alive in a world where someone can taste lies.
