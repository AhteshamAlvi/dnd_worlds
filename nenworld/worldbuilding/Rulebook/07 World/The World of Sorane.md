# The World of Sorane

[[00 Read Me First|Rulebook]] › World

**Everything on this page is a system-design addition.** The vault establishes a "Real world +" setting ([[World Building]]) with modern institutions ([[Nen in Society]]) and no geography. This file supplies an original map that *hosts* every established element — races, clans, [[Hollow]] caverns, [[Spirits]], [[Infernal]] outbreaks, [[Devil's Fruits]] — without contradicting any of it. Rename, cut, or redraw freely; the mechanical hooks survive any renaming.

Players know **their home region and rumors.** The rest is discovery ([[00 Read Me First]]).

## The shape of the world

**Sorane** is a wide world of five continents around the **Meridian Sea**, with two more layers stacked on it: the **Shelves** (high sky-lands above the archipelago winds) and the **Undervault** (the global deep caverns where Hollows form — [[Hollow]]). Modernity is real but uneven: Veldran cities run trains, phones, and licensing databases; the Hollowlands eat expeditions alive.

## Veldra — the modern center

Where default campaigns start. Schools teach Nen theory, licenses gate work, and the tension between *regulated power* and *everything older than regulation* drives daily life.

- **The Accord of Yth** — the supranational authority founded by the old kings, seated in the spire-city **Ythmar**. Sets licensing treaties, runs the [[Society Law and Licenses|Registry]]'s charter, and shelters the **[[Celestial Dragons]]** caste whose legal untouchability is the Accord's original sin. *Play here:* politics, diplomatic escort, the Gilded Bureau (Accord secret police) as patron or problem. *Threat it creates:* justice with a carve-out at the top.
- **Karuna Republic** — reform-minded republic; the friendliest place to be young and licensed. Home of **Sella Polytechnic of Applied Nen** and the **Civil Response Program** (licensed emergency-Nen service — hero work with paperwork). *Play:* exams, response calls, campus arcs ([[Campaign Frameworks]]).
- **Empire of Dazan** — conscription empire on Veldra's east; treats Nen users as strategic materiel, runs state training crucibles ([[Nen Growth]] crucible rules, institutionalized). *Play:* war arcs, defection stories, license reciprocity crises.
- **Clanlands of Homura** — mountain patchwork where the great bloodline [[Clans]] hold their valleys (Hyuga terraces, Yuki high passes, Kaguya bone-shrines; the [[Kurta]] survivors are *not* on any map, which is the point). Accord law runs thin; clan law runs deep. *Play:* clan intrigue, bloodline hunters, the [[Humans and Subtypes (Races)|trait]] economy at its ugliest.
- **Free City of Berrin** — port free-city, auction capital of everything that shouldn't be sold: relics, [[Devils Fruits (Rules)|fruits]], Spirit Tools, and — in the deep rings — people with traits. House **Nostrade** brokers; the **Sable Troupe** steals what brokers won't sell. *Play:* underworld campaigns, heists, the moral floor of the setting.

## Ostreva — the old world

Kingdom country: knight-orders, elf woods, ruins with things still in them. Modernity arrives by ferry, late.

- **The Marches of Bell** — a kingdom defended by **knight-orders of licensed Nen users** (squads with names, banners, and rivalries — guild-framework play). Nobility still owns too much of the sky.
- **The Eldenwood** — [[Elf]] country and [[Plantfolk]] groves under the **Verdant Pact** (elves, Plantfolk, and Awakened Beast clans jointly gatekeeping the old aura-dense sites). Training pilgrimages come here — so do poachers of everything sacred.
- **Duchy of Grimmen** — the demon-scarred frontier: land soaked in old grief where **Cursed [[Spirits]]** condense thick, watched by an exhausted chivalric order. Frieren-flavored long-history melancholy; the best ruins, the worst nights.
- **The Deep Doors** — Ostreva's dungeon-ruins (Magi-flavored): sealed complexes of pre-Accord Nen architecture, full of rule-effects that outlived their makers ([[Hatsu Design]] rule-effects as *terrain*). Chartered delves only — officially.

## Shoyan Archipelago — the free waters

Ten thousand islands, three jurisdictions, one bounty economy. [[Merfolk]] nations below (**the Coral Throne**), free ports on the water, **the Shelves** above ([[Sky People]], and higher, where nobody's invited, the [[Sky People|Angels]]).

*Play:* bounty campaigns (the Registry posts, free captains collect), smuggling, island-hopping exploration, the Coral Throne's cold diplomacy with the surface, Shelf-fall salvage (things fall from the sky here; some are people). *Threats:* pirate crews with elite captains, weather that answers to an ancient [[Elementals|Elemental]], and the Meridian's one hard rule — deep water belongs to things with more patience than you.

## Ashfall — fire and the pale north

The hard continent. South: volcanic belts where [[Infernal]] outbreaks cluster (the carrier insects breed in ash-country), watched by the **Lantern Watch** — the joint corps that answers Infernal ignitions, [[Vampires|Vampire]] nests, and monster incursions with rotating crews of licensed specialists ([[Campaign Frameworks]] response arcs). North: the **Pale Marches**, old forest under long nights, the acknowledged range of Vampire courts and at least one **Original**. Between them: mining states that pay triple for guard work and ask no questions twice.

## The Hollowlands — the closed frontier

The aura-dense southern landmass. Natural energy pools thick enough to pattern [[Elementals]]; [[Monsters]] grow old and strange; at least three of the nine **Tailed Beasts** range here between catastrophes. Registry law: entry by **Elite Charter or better**, and the Registry enforces it less to keep people out than to keep *records* of what goes in and doesn't come back. Aura-dense zone rules apply ([[Injury Recovery and Conditions]]): recovery doubles, minds bleed. *Play:* expedition arcs — the endgame's proving ground, and the only place [[Nen Growth|soft-capped]] masters still feel small.

## The Undervault — beneath everything

The deep caverns where [[Hollow]]s form, everywhere, under every continent. Breaches (**Garganta** events; mass **Kūmon** breaches for Gillian-class) are rare, tracked, and the single best argument the Registry has for its own existence: civilians can't even *see* what comes out. Breach-response is the campaign framework every region shares.

## The nine (GM reference)

The **Tailed Beasts** ([[Monsters]]) are scattered: three ranging the Hollowlands, one sleeping under the Meridian, one bound as a [[Humans and Subtypes (Races)|Jinchūriki]] by Dazan (the Accord pretends not to know), one worshipped as a mountain in Homura, three unaccounted for. Any of them is a campaign's *era*, not its encounter.

## Using the map

Every region above names: what players *do* there, which [[Campaign Frameworks|framework]] it runs natively, and what threat scales there. Start campaigns in one region; let the rest arrive as rumors, refugees, and freight. The world is big on purpose — discovery is the reward economy ([[Progression and Training]] pays TP for it).
