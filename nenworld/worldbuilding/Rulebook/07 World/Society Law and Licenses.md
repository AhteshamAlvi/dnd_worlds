# Society, Law, and Licenses

[[00 Read Me First|Rulebook]] › World

The rules layer of [[Nen in Society]]: Nen is public, taught in theory at school, licensed in practice, and monitored — "use of Nen abilities to cause harm is punished swiftly." Institutions below are design additions built to host exactly that lore.

## What ordinary people know

The player-safe primer lives in [[Awakening and the Path]]. Civic reality: everyone did the breathing exercises in school; everyone knows Nen users exist and are registered; **almost nobody can see aura** — which means the public's entire relationship with real Nen is *trust in the Registry*. That trust is the setting's load-bearing wall, and every campaign framework leans on it somewhere.

## The Registry

The treaty body (chartered by the Accord, operating nearly everywhere) that examines, licenses, and polices Nen use.

**License tiers:**

| Tier | Grants | Typical holder |
|---|---|---|
| **Provisional** | Supervised training; awakening under an accredited teacher | Students |
| **Licensed** | Work requiring Nen (guard, response, guide); technique use in public | Professional band |
| **Chartered** | Hatsu use in sanctioned work; bounty collection; **ability registration required** (below) | Established professionals |
| **Elite Charter** | Hollowlands entry; command of response crews; cross-border reciprocity | Elite band |
| **Crown Charter** | Registry authority itself: audits, warrants, exam design | A handful per region |

**Ability registration:** Chartered+ must file an ability's **name and class** (offense/defense/sensory/control/other) — *never* its mechanics or [[Conditions]]. The compromise is deliberate and politically alive: disclosure hawks want conditions on file; every working user knows a filed condition is a published death warrant ([[Running the Game]] — information is the real currency). Filed names are searchable by warrant. Yes, people file misleading names. Yes, that's a crime. Yes, everyone does it anyway.

**Exams** (framework in [[Campaign Frameworks]]): staged skill-challenges — written theory (INT Nen Theory), controlled technique demonstration (checks against fixed DCs per tier), and a live practicum the Registry is legally required to make survivable and does not always manage. Passing Licensed ≈ requires the [[Four Major Principles]] at rank I–II and Control 2.

## Law (the short table)

| Act | Standing |
|---|---|
| Awakening someone by [[Awakening and the Path|Initiation]] without accreditation | Felony ("aura assault") — the lore's taboo, codified |
| Bloodlust-Ren against the unawakened | Assault; against crowds, terrorism statutes |
| Unlicensed paid Nen work | Fines → revocation ladder |
| Unregistered Hatsu in public harm | The aggravating factor in any charge |
| [[Types and Affinity (Rules)|Manipulation]] of a person | The heavy crime — treated like poisoning + kidnapping; Soliciting-grade influence is where courts fight |
| Dueling | Legal in sanctioned circuits (below); illegal dueling is assault with stakes |
| Trait trafficking (bloodline harvesting, [[Marechi]] sale) | Capital crime under Accord law; thriving in Berrin anyway |
| Killing in defense against Hollows/Monsters | Lawful; the Watch exists so you rarely must |

Enforcement reality varies by region ([[The World of Sorane]]): Karuna prosecutes, Berrin invoices, the Clanlands never called it a crime, and against a [[Celestial Dragons|Celestial Dragon]] the law is a suggestion the Accord makes to itself.

## Work and money

Currency: **ryn**. Modest week ≈ 50 ryn. Illustrative rates:

| Work | Pay |
|---|---|
| Licensed guard week | 150–300 ryn |
| Response call (Watch/CRP per incident) | 100–500 by band |
| Chartered bounty: professional-band target | 2k–10k |
| Elite-band bounty | 50k+ and a reputation |
| Hollowlands expedition share | 10k–ruinous; survival not included |
| Yū-clinic limb regeneration | 20k+ (weeks of a master's pool — [[Nen Techniques (Rules)|Yū]] priced into the economy) |
| Devil's Fruit at Berrin auction, unidentified | 100k+; identified: an heirloom's ransom ([[Devils Fruits (Rules)]]) |

Sports and circuits: licensed dueling leagues, En-tag, transmuter exhibition circuits — legal fame paths ([[Nen in Society]]: "many sports have to do with Nen"). Athlete background + arena framework = a whole campaign.

## Institutions in play

- **The Lantern Watch** — response corps (Infernal ignitions, Vampire nests, breaches). Joining structures a campaign; calling them is the setting's 911 with a 40-minute ETA and a triage doctrine.
- **Civil Response Program (Karuna)** — licensed hero-work: publicly known responders with fan followings and byzantine liability rules.
- **Kembu Temples** — monastic Nen schools older than the Registry; teach for devotion, not ryn ([[Nen Growth]] master-teacher access, earned narratively).
- **Sella Polytechnic** — applied-Nen university: theory, forensics, the discipline of [[Hatsu Design|ability analysis]] as an academic field.
- **The Gilded Bureau** — the Accord's quiet service: audits Registry corruption, buries Celestial Dragon embarrassments, and offers PCs exactly the kind of work that costs more than it pays.
- **Guilds (Ostreva)** — chartered mercenary halls with boards, dues, and family; the Emberhall's board is a campaign generator ([[Campaign Frameworks]]).

## How Nen bends the ordinary (GM levers)

- **Medicine:** Yū clinics exist, are few, and are priced above (waiting lists = plot). Ordinary medicine still does most of the world's healing.
- **Law enforcement:** aura forensics (residue reads — [[Nen Techniques (Rules)|Gyō]] traces, DC 13–20 by age) makes Nen crime scenes *legible* to experts, which makes [[Zetsu]]-clean crews elite.
- **War:** Dazan doctrine treats elite users as strategic assets under treaty counting rules — an arms-control regime for people ([[Campaign Frameworks]] war arcs).
- **Exploration:** charters, survey bonds, and the Registry's grim ledger of the Hollowlands.
- **Commerce:** Shū-certified couriers, Conjurer-notarized contracts (a conjured seal is *visible* proof — [[Conjuration]]), In-swept boardrooms.
- **Faith:** Blessed [[Spirits]] worshipped locally; the Verdant Pact's sacred sites; theology quarrels over Construct souls and Angel divinity ([[Supernatural Races]]).
- **Class:** the real gap isn't wealth — it's *whose children get accredited teachers*. SPI is dealt at birth; access is dealt by everything else. That sentence is half the setting's politics.
