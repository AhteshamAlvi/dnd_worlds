# Campaign Frameworks

[[00 Read Me First|Rulebook]] › World

Ten ready structures. Each lists: premise, native region, band range, session rhythm, and seeds. All assume the [[00 Read Me First|first-arc awakening]] unless marked veteran. Mix freely — most long campaigns chain three or four.

## 1 · The Examination
*Fodder to Licensed, on the record.* — Karuna or any Registry hall · Tier 0 → novice · The default first arc.
**Rhythm:** written theory (INT checks with study prep), technique demonstrations (fixed-DC ladders from [[Society Law and Licenses]]), then the practicum: a live scenario (escort, search, holdout) where examiners watch *judgment*, not power. Awakening and [[Awakening and the Path|the Principles]] happen across prep chapters.
**Seeds:** a rival cohort with a Clan scion who's never been told no; a practicum that goes off-script because something real got in; an examiner who fails passing students for reasons that turn out to be right.

## 2 · The Tournament
*Brackets, betting, and rules with loopholes.* — Anywhere with a circuit · novice → elite · Veteran-friendly.
**Rhythm:** alternating prep/intel sessions and bouts. Publish the ruleset (ring-out, yield, no Manipulation of persons, medical [[Nen Techniques (Rules)|Yū]] on site) and let players lawyer it — tournament arcs are [[Hatsu Design]] showcases. Seed the bracket with two fights the party can't win fairly and one they shouldn't win at all.
**Seeds:** a betting ring fixing bouts via a Soliciting-grade [[Types and Affinity (Rules)|Manipulator]]; a masked entrant whose filed ability name is a lie; the sponsor's real prize is scouting for [[The World of Sorane|Dazan]].

## 3 · Bounty Work
*The Registry posts; free hands collect.* — Shoyan native; works anywhere · professional+ (Chartered).
**Rhythm:** pick target → investigate (the actual game: pattern, conditions, allies) → the take. Pay by band ([[Society Law and Licenses]]). The board teaches the setting's core lesson: **read the bounty's band before the fight does** ([[NPCs Creatures and Encounters]] threat math).
**Seeds:** a bounty whose "crimes" are a Registry cover; two crews, one target, first-collect law; a target who surrenders and is worth more alive to worse people.

## 4 · Breach Response
*Something opened under the market square.* — Universal (the Undervault is under everyone) · any band (breach class scales).
**Rhythm:** alarm → triage (civilians can't *see* [[Hollow]]s — evacuation is a Nen-user's job) → containment → the closing problem (Garganta don't shut politely). Kūmon events are war-scale set pieces with a Gillian clock.
**Seeds:** a breach that tracks a grieving household (Hollows follow desire); a Watch commander rationing crews between two towns; an Adjuchas that *talks*, and wants a deal.

## 5 · The Expedition
*Charter south. Come back written down.* — Hollowlands · elite+ · The endgame's proving ground.
**Rhythm:** sponsorship and supply clocks → travel legs (aura-dense zone rules: [[Injury Recovery and Conditions]]) → discovery sites (Elementals, ruins, things without files) → the ledger: what's worth what to whom. Growth rates spike out here ([[Nen Growth]] crucible) and so does the death rate; that symmetry *is* the frontier.
**Seeds:** a prior expedition's camp, intact, empty, warm; a Tailed Beast's migration crossing your only pass; a sponsor clause nobody read aloud.

## 6 · Faction Intrigue
*The Accord's peace, maintained by people like you.* — Ythmar/Accord space · professional+ · Social-forward.
**Rhythm:** patron tasking → information play ([[Social Exploration and Investigation]]: reads, leverage, favor-webs) → the moment the paper world and the power world touch. [[Celestial Dragons]] make justice structurally impossible at the top; intrigue campaigns are about what players do with that fact.
**Seeds:** a Dragon's "misplaced" purchase is a person; a Registry auditor asks the party to verify an Elite Charter's filed ability — quietly; Dazan treaty-counts elite users and one of yours just tipped the count.

## 7 · Underworld
*Berrin runs on everything the law can't hold.* — Free City of Berrin · any band · Grey-to-black.
**Rhythm:** score → crew prep ([[In]]/[[Zetsu]] make heists a Nen discipline) → execution → heat. The auction calendar is the city's heartbeat; the deep rings (trait trafficking) are its rot — decide session zero whether the party fights it, feeds it, or looks away.
**Seeds:** steal a [[Devils Fruits (Rules)|Devil's Fruit]] *before* it's eaten; a Sable Troupe member wants out and knows too much; the item on the block is somebody's sealed [[Supernatural Races|Spirit Tool]] — and it's awake.

## 8 · The War Arc
*Dazan moves; the map holds its breath.* — Veldra east · professional → master · Mass stakes, personal scenes.
**Rhythm:** the front is background weather (mass combat abstracted: named-band users decide engagements; armies decide *maps*); play runs missions — seize, deny, rescue, duel. Elite users under treaty-counting rules give war arcs their spy-thriller layer.
**Seeds:** an enemy crucible camp "graduating" conscript awakeners ([[Nen Growth]] read as atrocity); a bridge held by one master and the three days it buys; peace terms that trade a Jinchūriki like freight.

## 9 · The Investigation
*Aura leaves fingerprints.* — Karuna/Sella native · any band · Procedural.
**Rhythm:** scene forensics (Gyō-trace DCs, [[Society Law and Licenses]]) → theory-building (Nen Theory checks to deduce ability class and [[Conditions]] from effects — the game's applied science) → confrontation where knowing their Condition *is* the weapon. Protect hidden info per [[Running the Game]] — mysteries die of leaked mechanics.
**Seeds:** a locked-room death with no aura residue at all ([[Zetsu]]-clean, or worse: Nen-null); serial thefts of worthless heirlooms (a [[Humans and Subtypes (Races)|Fullbringer]] shopping for history); a licensed healer whose patients recover *too* completely.

## 10 · The Deep Doors
*Ostreva's ruins remember their makers' rules.* — Ostreva · professional+ · Dungeon logic, Nen physics.
**Rhythm:** delve charters → the Doors themselves: pre-Accord complexes running centuries-old [[Hatsu Design|rule-effects]] as architecture ("the third hall admits one person at a time"; "nothing spoken here is heard outside"). Map the rules, exploit the rules, survive the custodian constructs. Loot: relics, texts ([[Nen Growth]] classical manuals), sealed Tools.
**Seeds:** a Door that opens only for an affinity nobody in the party has; a custodian still following its dead maker's last order; a rival delve crew triggering rules blind.

---

**Chaining guidance:** Examination → Bounty/Response → (band up) → Tournament or Investigation → Intrigue/War → Expedition is the "grand tour" spine that shows the whole setting in one campaign. Every framework names which band it stresses; check the party against [[NPCs Creatures and Encounters]] before committing an arc — the math is load-bearing and it does not forgive optimism.
