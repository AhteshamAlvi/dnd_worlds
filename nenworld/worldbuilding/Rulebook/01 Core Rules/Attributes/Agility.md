# Agility (AGI)

[[00 Read Me First|Rulebook]] › Core Rules › [[Attributes]]

**Agility is whole-body quickness and control — how well you move, dodge, and keep your feet.** It is the twin of [[Dexterity|DEX]]: AGI moves your *body*, DEX moves your *hands*.

## What AGI governs

- **Evasion** — your passive defense that attacks roll against ([[Combat Core]]):
  > **Evasion = 10 + AGI mod** (+2 shield/style, +2 for the Dodge reaction)
- **Reflex saves** — dodging area effects, blasts, collapsing ground, and anything you beat by getting out of the way ([[Attributes and Skills]]).
- **Base Speed Rating (SR)** — your movement band ([[Scale Speed and Magnitude]]). A human is SR 1 by default, **SR 2 if AGI 14+** (or Athletics-trained). SR then climbs with training, Drive aura, and [[Nen Techniques (Rules)|Chū]]. AGI sets the floor everything else builds on.
- **Combat movement, Dash, disengage, balance, tumbling, squeezing, falling safely** ([[Combat Core]]).
- **Grapple escape** — a grappled character may resist/break with **AGI (Acrobatics)** instead of STR ([[Combat Core]]).
- **Chases** — the sprint/parkour leg of a chase is AGI ([[Injury Recovery and Conditions]]).
- **Acrobatics** and **Stealth** are the AGI skills ([[Attributes and Skills]]).

## Interactions

- **AGI vs DEX** — dodging a strike is AGI; landing one, or disarming a trap, is DEX.
- **Chū** can temporarily raise AGI (aura density → +AGI score, rank-capped), which also feeds Speed Rating — the engine behind "non-Enhancers reach Mach speeds" ([[Nen Techniques (Rules)]], [[Aura Density and Concentration]]).
- **Speed gaps** are resolved on SR, not raw AGI — a 2+ SR advantage grants +2 to hit and +2 EVA against the slower side; 5+ is auto-hit/uncatchable ([[Scale Speed and Magnitude]]).

## The Agility scale (1–30)

| Score | Mod | What this AGI looks like |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | −5 | Can barely stand unaided; falls at a nudge. |
| 2 | −4 | Profoundly clumsy or infirm; needs support to walk uneven ground. |
| 3 | −4 | Still badly unsteady, but can shuffle short distances unaided. |
| 4 | −3 | Very slow and unsteady; trips easily. |
| 5 | −3 | A little steadier, but still dodges nothing worth calling a dodge. |
| 6 | −2 | Stiff, plodding; last to react. |
| 7 | −2 | Slightly less stiff, but still slow to move when it counts. |
| 8 | −1 | A bit slow and graceless. |
| 9 | −1 | Mildly clumsy; gets by without incident. |
| 10 | +0 | Average adult — jogs, catches a tossed ball (SR 1). |
| 11 | +0 | A touch above average — dodges a slow swing without thinking (SR 1). |
| 12 | +1 | Light on the feet; a decent dancer or amateur athlete. |
| 13 | +1 | Solidly nimble; a step above the amateur crowd. |
| 14 | +2 | Trained mover — martial artist, gymnast, skirmisher (SR 2). |
| 15 | +2 | Seasoned mover; reads terrain and openings at speed (SR 2). |
| 16 | +3 | Exceptional reflexes; hard to pin. |
| 17 | +3 | Elite reflexes; slips through crowds like they aren't there. |
| 18 | +4 | Peak human — Olympic sprinter/gymnast. |
| 19 | +4 | World-class — sidesteps a knife in a phone-booth. |
| 20 | +5 | The human limit of speed and balance; records that stand. |
| 21 | +5 | Just past the human limit of speed and balance. Only Nen-tempering, a racial gift, a Mutation, or similarly special circumstances reach here. |
| 22 | +6 | Superhuman: outruns a motorcycle. |
| 23 | +6 | Superhuman: dodges arrows at range, runs across taut wire. |
| 24 | +7 | Sidesteps gunfire at conversational distance. |
| 25 | +7 | Reacts to an ambush before the attacker finishes committing. |
| 26 | +8 | A moving blur to bystanders. |
| 27 | +8 | Crosses a plaza before a shout finishes. |
| 28 | +9 | Faster than the eye can track. |
| 29 | +9 | Moves between one heartbeat and the next; onlookers see only the result. |
| 30 | +10 | Apex mortal quickness — motion that reads as teleportation to anyone slower (pairs with very high SR). |
