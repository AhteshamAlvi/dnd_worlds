# Character Creation

[[00 Read Me First|Rulebook]] › Characters

Creation builds an **ordinary person**: no Nen, no abilities, no fruit powers (design commitment #1). Skills and gear come from the life they've lived. Power comes later, from play — which means creation's real product is the *reasons* this person will chase power and the *life* that will shape their [[Hatsu]]. Everything here should take a table one session.

## Step 1 — Concept and race

One sentence: who are you and what do you want? Then pick a race **from the campaign's available list** — the GM restricts by starting region and logistics ([[Race Rules Overview]]; Giants, [[Hollow]]s, and other absurd-progression races are GM-only by default). Race sets attribute adjustments and caps, Scale Factor, base SR, traits, lifespan — never personality, profession, morality, or Nen type.

## Step 2 — Attributes

**Standard array** — assign 15, 14, 13, 12, 11, 10, 10, 9, 8, 8 across the ten attributes ([[Attributes and Skills]]) — or **point buy** (all start 8; 24 points; 1 pt/step to 13, 2 pts/step to 16). Then apply racial adjustments (creation cap 18 after race; SPI creation cap 16 for humans).

Tell players what SPI is ("innate Nen talent") and nothing about what it does. Where they put it is already a story bet. **A quiet note for the GM only:** once these characters awaken, **CON** will set their aura pool and its lifetime ceiling, **VIT** their aura recovery and Body HP, **DEX** their aura execution and all accuracy, and **SPI** their odds on the rare make-or-break Nen manifestations ([[Aura Statistics]], [[Nen Manifestation]]). None of that is visible at creation — the fodder phase runs on the physical and social stats — but the array a player picks is the Nen build they'll grow into. Don't tell them; let it be discovered.

## Step 3 — Background (the life so far)

Pick one; it grants **3 Trained skills, 1 save proficiency, gear, and a hook** ([[Attributes and Skills]] for the skill list):

| Background | Skills | Save | Gear & hook |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Street Kid** | Stealth, Streetwise, Sleight of Hand | Reflex | Knife, lockpicks, a debt someone still holds |
| **Laborer** | Athletics, Endurance, Craft | Fortitude (CON) | Good tools, guild card, a body that remembers |
| **Soldier / Militia** | Weapons (group), Perception, Endurance | Fortitude (VIT) | Service weapon, old armor, a war that isn't done with you |
| **Scholar** | Academics, Nen Theory, Investigation | Will | Books, a mentor's letter, a question you can't put down |
| **Clan Scion** | Academics, Persuasion, one clan-flavored skill | Will | Heirloom, the name itself (doors open *and* close), duty |
| **Merchant** | Persuasion, Insight, Streetwise | Will | Ledger, seed money (+100 ryn), a rival house |
| **Wilds-born** | Survival, Animal Handling, Athletics | Fortitude (CON) | Traveling kit, a beast that knows you, why you left |
| **Performer** | Performance, Acrobatics, Deception | Reflex | Instrument/props, a name on some circuit, an admirer |
| **Acolyte** | Academics, Insight, Medicine | Will | Order's raiment, sanctuary rights, a vow you already keep |
| **Criminal** | Deception, Stealth, Sleight of Hand | Reflex | Burner identity, a crew that wants you back, heat |
| **Medic** | Medicine, Insight, Endurance | Will | Field kit, clinic contacts, the one you couldn't save |
| **Athlete** | Athletics, Acrobatics, Composure | Reflex | Sponsor gear, local fame, the loss that still stings |

## Step 4 — Formative experience

One line about the moment that bent your life ("the flood year," "my sister's Initiation went wrong," "I saw a licensed user stop a riot alone"). Grants: **+1 Trained skill or attack proficiency**, **one contact** (named NPC), and — write it down — **your reason to awaken.** The GM will use it in the first arc; that's a promise, not a threat.

## Step 5 — Free proficiencies

**+2 Trained picks** (skills or attack proficiencies; or trade both picks to raise one Trained skill to Expert). Humans get +1 more pick (racial).

## Step 6 — Derived numbers

- **Body HP** = VIT × 3 × SF (SF ×1 for almost all playable races)
- **Evasion** = 10 + AGI mod
- **Speed Rating** = race base (human 1; 2 if AGI 14+ *or* Athletics trained; see race entries)
- **Initiative** = PER mod · **Passive Perception** = 10 + PER mod + prof
- **Saves** = attribute checks; mark background/race proficiencies
- **Aura statistics: none.** Leave the whole [[Aura Statistics]] block blank. It gets filled in ink the day the character awakens — at the table.

## Step 7 — Equipment and money

Background gear + **200 ryn** (see [[Society Law and Licenses]] for the economy; a week's modest living ≈ 50 ryn). Mundane armor: light (+1 Soak), heavy (+2 Soak, −1 EVA), shield (+2 EVA). Weapons: [[Combat Core]] table. Buy freely; none of it will matter in a year, and all of it matters enormously right now — armor is for the fodder phase, and the fodder phase is real.

## Step 8 — Nen status (GM bookkeeping)

The character is **Tier 0 — Ordinary** ([[The Nen Path]]). Now, privately:
- If the player chose **discussed pick** for affinity, record it sealed.
- Otherwise pre-roll their d100 type and d20 variation ([[Types and Affinity (Rules)]]) and seal both.
- Note their CON (their eventual aura ceiling and growth pace) and SPI (their manifestation edge) — [[Nen Growth]], [[Nen Manifestation]].
- Optional: 1-in-1000 humans are Nen-null ([[Mutations]]) — **only with player buy-in**, it's a different game for them ([[Race Rules Overview]], Mutations section).

## Step 9 — Ties

Minimum, written on the sheet: **a goal** (what power is *for*), **a flaw** (what will make your Vows expensive), **two relationships** (one warm, one barbed), and **one link to another PC.** These aren't color — the Sincerity Audit ([[Conditions Vows and Risk (Rules)]]) prices Vows against them, and abilities are *grown* from them ([[Developing a Nen Ability]]).

## Step 10 — Table check

GM reads the party as a set: shared reason to be together, at least two ties crossing between PCs, everyone's awakening-reason usable in the first arc. Fix gaps now. Then start playing — the fodder phase is short, but it's the foundation the whole campaign's Nen grows from.

## Variant: veteran start

For campaigns skipping the fodder phase: build as above, then apply [[Nen Growth]] retroactively — GM sets in-fiction years of training; typical **professional start**: awakened, AP 600–1k, Ren II–III, Control 2–3, two intermediate techniques at I–II, Hatsu II with one designed form, and 10 TP already spent on the above. The affinity reveal still deserves its scene — run the Water Divination as a flashback.

## Different races, one party

All PCs start Nen-less at base level, so racial spread at creation is bounded by design: the biggest legal creation gaps are ~+4 attribute points and a trait (a [[Fanalis]] scion hits harder than a human scholar — and the scholar's higher CON may mean a far deeper aura ceiling once both awaken, invisible for now). The math converges further as Nen grows: aura statistics don't care about race except where a trait says so, and traits are priced ([[Race Rules Overview]]). What does *not* converge: lifespan, social treatment, and biology — play those as the world reacting, not as numbers.
