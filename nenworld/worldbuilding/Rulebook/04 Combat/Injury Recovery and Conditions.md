# Injury, Recovery, and Conditions

[[00 Read Me First|Rulebook]] › Combat

The lethality dial (per the design brief): **between dangerous and gritty.** There are real buffers before death — Dying, injuries, GM flow — but no plot armor. Players who seek death find it; players who keep their wits, retreat, and negotiate survive things far beyond their weight.

## Body HP

> **Body HP = VIT × 3 × Scale Factor** ([[Scale Speed and Magnitude]])

Human range 24–60. Body HP is *meat*: it never scales with aura, training only nudges it (VIT raises), and no human ever tanks a P 500 blow on flesh. Guard is the scaling defense; Body is what's left when Guard fails.

- **Bloodied** (half Body): no penalty, but it's *visible* — abilities and NPC behaviors key off it, wounds bleed, fear checks for bystanders.
- **0 Body:** **Dying** (below). Nonlethal intent: unconscious-stable instead.

## Dying and death

At 0 Body HP you fall Dying. On each of your turns: **death save** — d20 + VIT mod: 10+ success, else failure. **Three failures: dead. Three successes: stable** (unconscious, 1 HP after 1d4 hours). Natural 20: wake at 1 HP. Damage taken while Dying = 1 failure (2 if the hit's Power ≥ your max Body).

- **Massive damage:** a single hit dealing ≥ 2× max Body sends you Dying with **1 failure banked + a Severe injury** — never instant death from HP loss alone (design brief: no insta-kills). The *only* instant kill is a coup de grâce against the helpless ([[Combat Core]]) or brain destruction ([[Nen Techniques (Rules)|Yū]] can't fix that; nothing can).
- **Players roll their own death saves, in the open.** The table should hold its breath honestly.
- **Stabilize:** Medicine DC 13 (action), any healing, or the fight ending with a merciful victor.

## Injuries

Roll d10 on this table when: dropped to 0 Body, **or** hit for more than half max Body in one blow, **or** a called shot lands on the unguarded:

| d10 | Injury | Effect | Heals |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1–3 | **Battered** | −1 all d20s | 1 week rest, or trivial [[Nen Techniques (Rules)|Yū]] |
| 4–6 | **Deep wound** | −2 all d20s; one physical attribute −2 | Treated + 2 weeks; Yū at internal rates |
| 7–8 | **Broken** | Limb unusable / ribs: no Dash, Concentration at disadvantage | Set (Medicine DC 13) + 4 weeks; Yū after setting |
| 9 | **Severe** | Lost eye, fingers, organ damage: permanent −2 to a linked stat/skill | Only Yū regeneration (50:1) or master healers |
| 10 | **Maiming** | Lost limb | Only Yū regeneration over daily sessions ([[Nen Techniques (Rules)]]) |

Injuries stack. This table is the game's tax on fighting up a band — you can survive an elite, but you'll wear the receipt.

## Strain (aura overuse)

**Strain** is nervous-system damage from pushing aura past sane limits. Each point: **−1 all d20s and −10% AO.** Sources: crucible training, [[Nen Techniques (Rules)|En]] overrun, Chū past its cap, Overdraw, some abilities' tolls.

- Recover 1 Strain per full rest day (no Nen use); Yū can't touch it — it isn't a wound, it's *wear*.
- **At 5 Strain:** collapse — Aura Sickness: no Nen for a week, all the leaking misery of a fresh awakener.

## Aura exhaustion and Overdraw

- **0 AP: Exhausted** — disadvantage on everything, no techniques, SR −1, visibly leaking; recover via [[Aura Statistics]] rates (Zetsu triples, at the price of being defenseless).
- **Overdraw (desperation):** at 0 AP you may burn Body HP as aura, **1 HP : 2 aura**, +1 Strain per round doing it. This is the lore's "temporarily exceed your limits" made mechanical — the surge that wins the day and hospitalizes the hero. It cannot exceed your AO per round, and the GM should narrate it as the body cannibalizing itself, because it is.
- **Desperation Surge (1/arc):** at a moment of true emotional extremity, +50% AO for the scene; afterward gain 2 Strain and lose access to the surge until the next arc. Pairs with the Risk rules ([[Conditions Vows and Risk (Rules)]]).

## Natural recovery

- **Body HP:** VIT mod/day (min 1); ×2 under real care (Medicine DC 13, safe bed).
- **Blood loss** narratives: track as Battered until fluids + a day; [[Nen Techniques (Rules)|Chū]] doubles it (the lore's water clause).
- **Aura:** [[Aura Statistics]] recovery table. Calm matters: the hunted heal slower — by design.

## Condition glossary

| Condition | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Frightened** | Disadvantage on checks/attacks while source visible; can't approach it |
| **Paralyzed** | No actions/moves/reactions; melee hits vs you at advantage |
| **Numbed** | −2 all d20s; Strike halved |
| **Prone** | Melee vs you +2; your attacks −2; half move to stand |
| **Grappled / Restrained** | No move / + no reactions, attacks vs you at advantage ([[Combat Core]]) |
| **Blinded** | Attacks at disadvantage; EVA −4 vs unseen (En negates entirely — [[Nen Techniques (Rules)]]) |
| **Exhausted** | Disadvantage on everything (0 AP state, forced marches) |
| **Flat-footed** | No reactions, no Allocation; Ten shroud only (surprise) |
| **Marked** | A tracker ability holds your position (no hiding from that user) |
| **Leaking** | Fresh-awakener state: −2 all, aura visible to any user ([[Awakening and the Path]]) |

## Environmental hazards

| Hazard | Rule |
|---|---|
| Fire (ordinary) | P 10/round exposed; P 30 engulfed. Guard soaks it — walking through fire is a Nen party trick |
| Falls | Impact rules in [[Scale Speed and Magnitude]] (speed = 5 m/s per second, cap 55) |
| Suffocation | CON score in rounds of effort (×2 prepared); then Dying |
| Poison/disease | CON save vs listed DC; on fail, listed track. [[Nen Techniques (Rules)|Jū]] can reject *Nen-borne* affliction only |
| Extreme cold/heat | CON save DC 13/hour or 1 Strain-equivalent fatigue ([[Race Rules Overview|races]] modify: Yuki, Merfolk, Plantfolk…) |
| Aura-dense zones (Hollowlands, sacred sites) | Recovery ×2 but WIL DC 13/day or emotional bleed — see [[The World of Sorane]] |

## Chases and escapes

1. **Speed check:** SR gap 2+ in open ground = the faster side simply decides the outcome ([[Scale Speed and Magnitude]]).
2. Otherwise best-of-3 opposed legs: AGI (sprint/parkour), CON (endurance), WIS (route/prediction) — terrain picks which; each leg won by the quarry adds separation, each lost closes it.
3. Nen layer: Zetsu drops you off the aura map (then it's mundane tracking); En sweeps flush hiders; Drive spends pool for raw gap — and a pursuer who empties their pool to catch you arrives Exhausted. Let them do the math.

## Social and psychological pressure

Not a subsystem — the same engine: Intimidation vs Composure, bloodlust-Ren vs the [[Nen Techniques (Rules)|Ten]]-modified save, Insight vs Deception in negotiation, with stakes agreed before the roll ([[Social Exploration and Investigation]]). The one hard rule: **no roll makes a PC act against their player's choice** — social results move NPCs, information, and circumstances; players keep the wheel of their own characters. Manipulation-type Nen is the deliberate, expensive exception, and it has an entire price list.
