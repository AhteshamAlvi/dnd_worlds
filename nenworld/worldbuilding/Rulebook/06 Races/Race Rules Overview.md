# Race Rules Overview

[[00 Read Me First|Rulebook]] › Races

How the vault's [[Race|race taxonomy]] becomes table rules. Three companion files hold the entries: [[Humans and Subtypes (Races)]], [[Peoples (Races)]], [[Supernatural Races]].

## What a race entry contains

**Attribute adjustments** (net +3 to +5 points for playable races) · **attribute caps** (default 20; races move specific caps) · **Scale Factor** and **base SR** ([[Scale Speed and Magnitude]]) · **traits** (1–3, priced by the grades below) · **senses** · **lifespan & maturation** · **type leaning** (a d100 shift in [[Types and Affinity (Rules)]], never a lock) · **social standing** (how the world treats them — as playable as any number).

**Trait grades** (design yardstick used across all entries): *Minor* ≈ 2 attribute points (a sense, a resistance, a tool) · *Major* ≈ 4 (a natural weapon set, an environment mastery, a learning edge) · *Defining* ≈ 6+ (flight, a body of water, an extra sense layer — usually paired with a real vulnerability or GM-gating).

## The balance position (honest version)

The races are **not** equal, and the lore forbids pretending they are. Balance comes from four real mechanisms instead:

1. **Everyone starts Nen-less at base level** (design brief). No racial Nen, no abilities, no fruit powers at creation. A [[Fanalis]] teenager out-punches a human soldier; neither can touch aura yet.
2. **Creation packages are bounded** (+3 to +5 net attribute points, priced traits). The spread at session 1 is real but modest.
3. **Aura doesn't care about tonnage.** AP/AO/Control grow by [[Nen Growth]] rules for every race alike; only explicit traits touch them. Since endgame power is overwhelmingly aura, races converge as campaigns mature — bodies are a head start, not a destiny. (This is the lore's own position: [[Race]] — "none of these races are inherently tied to Nen"; [[Humans]] — humans hold their own *because* everything is built.)
4. **The restricted list.** Some races are a GM headache by construction — absurd progression curves, party-breaking logistics, or power floors above the campaign. They're statted for NPCs and *possible* as PCs, but only by explicit GM opt-in.

## Availability tiers

**Open (any campaign):** Human and human subtypes (clan traits priced individually), [[Beastfolk]] (Marked, Half-beast, Full), [[Elf]], Dwarf, [[Orckin]] and Goblinkin, [[Merfolk]] (fish-tailed and Fishman), [[Fairy]], [[Plantfolk]] (not Treants), [[Sky People]] (not Angels), [[Variant Tribes]].

**Regional (open if the campaign starts where they live):** [[Merfolk|Imuchakk]], deep-desert and high-sky variants, specific [[Race Rules Overview|clan]] homelands — the GM's starting-region list decides ([[The World of Sorane]]).

**Restricted (GM opt-in, one per party at most):** [[Fanalis]] (package strength), Saiyan (Zenkai progression is deliberately absurd — see entry), turned [[Vampires|Vampire]], lucid [[Infernal]], sapient [[Constructs|Construct]], [[Beastfolk|Winged Beastfolk and Shifters]] (flight/form logistics), [[Humans|Jinchūriki]] (a campaign in themselves), Celestial Dragon (a *social* nuke), Nen-null [[Mutations|Mutant]] (a different game — entry in the Humans file).

**GM-only (NPC races):** [[Giant]]s, all [[Hollow]] grades and Arrancar, [[Spirits]] (Blessed/Cursed), [[Elementals]], [[Sky People|Angels]], Original and Made-Original [[Vampires]], [[Monsters]] and Awakened Beasts, Treants, Lunarians. Reasons are always one of: power floor above campaign start, progression curve that outpaces a party (the design-brief examples: Giants, Hollows), or a nature that fights the premise (a Cursed Spirit PC is a hostage negotiation with the initiative order).

## Culture is not biology

Every entry separates **body** (inherited: attributes, senses, SF) from **culture** (learned: use [[Character Creation]] backgrounds for it). An Orckin raised in a human port city takes the Merchant background as naturally as anyone; the entry's "Orckin societies run on oaths" describes *societies*, not genes. No race entry ever sets personality, profession, morality, or Nen type — the lore is explicit and so is this book. Racial *leanings* on the type roll are ±10 at most and never reach certainty.

## Mixed ancestry

Common where peoples mingle ([[Merfolk]] interbreed freely; [[Variant Tribes]] are cross-fertile with baseline humans; half-elves walk every port).

> **Rule:** take one parent race's full mechanics; take one *Minor-grade* trait (or a cosmetic marker) from the other. Attribute caps: the friendlier of the two, per attribute. Clan bloodline traits follow clan rules ([[Humans and Subtypes (Races)]]) — most breed true only within the listed line; a half-line scion carries the trait at reduced grade or not at all (GM + player decide which story they're telling).

## Lifespan and maturation (reference)

| Race | Mature | Lifespan |
|---|---|---|
| Human | 16–18 | ~80–100 (Nen-tempered: +decades; [[Nen Techniques (Rules)|Ten]] slows aging) |
| Elf | ~25 | Centuries |
| Dwarf | ~25 | ~300 |
| Orckin / Goblinkin | 14 / 12 | ~70 / ~60 |
| Beastfolk | varies (12–20) | ~60–90 |
| Merfolk / Fishman | ~18 | ~120 |
| Fairy | ~10 | ~150 |
| Plantfolk | ~30 (from seed) | Centuries; Treants: ages of the world |
| Sky People | ~18 | ~100; Angels: unmeasured |
| Vampire / Spirit / Elemental / Hollow | — | Unaging (each with its own ends) |

Long-lived PCs and mortal parties: the numbers don't need balancing; the *table* needs the conversation. The one mechanical note: lifespan never changes training rates — a 300-year-old elf novice trains at [[Nen Growth]] rates like anyone (their advantage is having *started* long ago, which is a background question, not a discount).
