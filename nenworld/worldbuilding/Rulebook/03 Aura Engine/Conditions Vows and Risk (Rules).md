# Conditions, Vows, and Risk (Rules)

[[00 Read Me First|Rulebook]] › Aura Engine

The lore's power economy ([[Conditions]], [[Vows and Limitations]], [[Risk]]) is how anyone punches above their magnitude. This chapter prices it. One sentence holds the whole design: **Conditions buy *shape*, Vows buy *power*, Risk buys *moments* — and sincerity is the currency behind all three.**

## Conditions — unbreakable parameters

Conditions are the equivalent-exchange terms built into an ability. They cannot be broken (the ability simply doesn't work outside them) and they cannot be cheesed — the user's own internal locus of equivalence enforces them ([[Conditions]]).

Mechanically, Conditions grant **Effect Points (EP)** to an ability's design budget ([[Hatsu Design]]):

| Grade | The constraint is… | EP granted | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Minor** (+2 EP) | A real procedure or narrow cost | +2 | Must speak the ability's name; must touch the target with the right hand; costs a splash of your blood |
| **Major** (+5 EP) | A genuine tactical limitation | +5 | Only works on targets you've touched this scene; requires a physical medium you must retrieve; usable once per day; you feel every wound it inflicts |
| **Defining** (+10 EP) | The ability's whole identity | +10 | Only works on people who have lied to you; requires the target's freely-given true name; only inside a space you've slept in for a week |

- **Activation Requirements** are Conditions that gate *whether* it fires (grade by how hard they are to arrange). **Continuous requirements** (it ends the moment you stop humming, lose eye contact, release the hand sign) add +2 EP to whatever grade they'd otherwise be.
- **Tolls** are Conditions that only *cost* (pain, a day of your memory, a week of numbness in the arm): graded by real story-weight, same EP values.
- **Optional-boost procedures** (the [[Ryomen Sukuna|Sukuna]] pattern): telegraphing procedures the user *may* perform — pointing, tracing, a chant, a hand sign. Each performed step grants **+25% Power on that use** (max +100%), and each *gives the enemy real information* (direction, timing: they gain +2 EVA or a free reaction against it). Steps must genuinely telegraph or they grant nothing.

**Redundancy clause:** at Hatsu V a user may retire one Minor Condition per ability as their skill outgrows it ([[Conditions]] lore: "occasionally rendered redundant by high proficiency"), refunding its EP.

## Vows (Limitations) — breakable oaths, multiplied power

A Vow is a sworn rule the user *could* break — and that's exactly why it pays more than a Condition ([[Vows and Limitations]]: "Limitations seem to strengthen an ability to a greater degree"). A Vow multiplies the ability's **Power output** (or another named parameter):

| Vow grade | The rule costs the character… | Multiplier |
|---|---|---|
| **Light** | Something real but cheap | ×1.25 — "only after sundown" |
| **Serious** | A standing lifestyle constraint | ×1.5 — "I never strike first"; "only on Thursdays" |
| **Grave** | Identity-level restriction | ×2 — "only against my clan's killers"; "I will never use this to save my own life" |
| **Defining sacrifice** | A permanent capability, forever | ×3 — giving up an entire category's use or potential |

**Punishment riders** multiply the Vow's own multiplier: *"if I break this, I lose my Nen"* ×1.5; *"if I break this, I die"* ×2. (A Grave vow with a death rider: ×2 × 2 = ×4.) The punishment is real and self-executing — the GM owes the player its enforcement.

**Narrow-parameter rule.** A Vow whose entire benefit lands on **one parameter** (travel speed *only*, range *only*, duration *only* — never damage) counts one grade higher and is exempt from the stacking cap, to a maximum of **×10 on that parameter**. This is reverse-engineered from the flagship canon example: Sukuna's sacrifice of all Manipulation for ×10 slash *travel speed* ([[Ryomen Sukuna]]) — a Defining sacrifice, narrowed to one parameter, hitting the ×10 ceiling exactly. Max **two** narrow vows per ability.

**Stacking cap (design addition, anti-runaway):** the product of all Vow multipliers affecting an ability's overall Power may not exceed **×5**. Beyond that, the user's own locus of equivalence stops believing the exchange — the lore's internal-fairness clause doing balance work. [[Balance and Math]] shows why ×5 is the ceiling the action economy tolerates.

**Breaking a Vow:** the boost is gone — permanently for that clause; if the Vow made the ability possible at all, the ability is gone; any punishment executes immediately. A broken clause can never be re-sworn (your own depths no longer believe you).

## Risk — real stakes, in the moment

Self-endangerment amplifies Nen at the moment of use ([[Risk]]):

- **Built-in Risk** (designed into the ability — it burns your own Body HP, blinds you while active, can only be used at Bloodied): priced as Conditions above, *and* while the danger is live the ability runs at **+25% Power**.
- **Situational Risk** (GM-adjudicated, that use only): genuinely letting the enemy strike first, fighting at Dying's edge, standing inside your own blast: **×1.5 that use**. The danger must be real — if the GM wouldn't actually let it kill you, it pays nothing.
- **Emotion:** genuine emotional resonance (the ability's theme meeting its moment) grants **advantage** on the rolls involved, GM's call. Emotion is a spotlight lever, not a math lever — kept off the multiplier stack so the audit stays about *stakes*, not performance.

## The Sincerity Audit (GM procedure)

Run every proposed Condition/Vow through four questions — **secretly**; the answers are yours:

1. **Teeth:** Will this actually constrain or cost them in play, at least once per arc?
2. **Bite-back:** Would the player accept me enforcing it at the worst possible moment?
3. **Truth:** Does it fit who the character is — would *this person* swear *this*? (An oath the character finds meaningless grants nothing, however severe it sounds.)
4. **Frequency:** "Only on Thursdays" in a game where I control the calendar — will Thursdays actually be rare?

Any "no" → the clause is **cosmetic: it grants ×1 / +0 EP.** Tell the player *that it grants nothing*, not *why* — let them iterate in-fiction. Examples for calibration:

| Proposal | Ruling |
|---|---|
| "Only usable against enemies" | Cosmetic (when else?) — ×1 |
| "Only usable against people who outnumber me" | Serious ×1.5 (real, checkable, will bind) |
| "I must announce the ability's true name" (name is a secret tell) | Minor Condition +2 EP; becomes Major if the name reveals its weakness |
| "I'll die if this ever kills an innocent" | Grave ×2 with death rider ×2 — if and only if the campaign can put innocents in the blast |
| "Costs me one happy memory per use, chosen by the GM" | Major toll +5 EP, and yes, the GM collects |

## Priority rules

1. An unmet **Condition** beats everything: the ability does not function. 
2. A broken **Vow** resolves before any other effect of the action completes.
3. Multipliers apply in this order: base Power × Efficiency ([[Types and Affinity (Rules)]]) × Vows × optional-boost steps × situational Risk. Round at the end.
4. Nothing in this chapter modifies d20 accuracy — power economy touches Power, cost, range, duration, and parameters only ([[Scale Speed and Magnitude]]).

## Worked example — one blast, three ways

Sera, Emitter (AO 200, Control 4 ×1.25, Hatsu III), fires her bolt with 100 committed Fuel:

- **Plain:** P = 100 × 100% = **100**. Pool cost 125.
- **Conditioned:** she adds a chant + palm-point (two optional-boost steps, +50%, enemy gets +2 EVA vs it): P = **150**, same cost.
- **Vowed:** she has sworn *"I never fire the bolt at anyone who hasn't seen my face"* (Serious ×1.5) with no rider: P = 100 × 1.5 = **150** always, no telegraph — but the day she snipes a stranger from darkness, the clause dies forever.
- **All of it, in extremis:** vow ×1.5, both boost steps ×1.5, standing in her own blast radius ×1.5 → P = 100 × 1.5 × 1.5 × 1.5 ≈ **340**. One number multiplied three times; that's the whole arithmetic.
